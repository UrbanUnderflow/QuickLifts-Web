# Pulse Writing Doctrine

## Serious Error: Negation-Led Corrective Contrast

Pulse copy states the intended truth directly. Never explain an idea through a
negation-led correction such as "not X, but Y", "X is not Y; it is Z",
"X does not do Y; it does Z", or a defensive qualifier such as "it does not
replace Y."

This applies to athlete-facing copy, educational content, product writing,
specifications, and recommendations. Rewrite the sentence around the positive
principle, mechanism, or action. Safety prohibitions and literal validation
errors are the only exceptions.

## Smart Middle Schooler Voice

Every athlete-facing and educational line should sound natural when spoken to
a smart 13-year-old. Keep the idea intelligent and make the language concrete.
Name thoughts, feelings, choices, people, and moments the athlete can picture.
Avoid abstract performance-copy phrases such as "strengthen your state",
"shift your state", "regulate your system", "access your focus", "recognize
your pattern", and "create it on purpose." Never sound clinical, academic,
corporate, mystical without meaning, or childish.

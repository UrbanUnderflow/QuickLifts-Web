# PulseCheck to AuntEdna to Hampton Playbook

## Overview
This playbook outlines the procedure for navigating the PulseCheck process through AuntEdna and into Hampton. The focus is on mapping severity buckets to ensure precise triage, enhancing the efficiency of our process and increasing the success rate of issue management.

## Severity Buckets
- **Critical**: Immediate attention required, potential for high impact if not resolved quickly.
- **High**: Significant impact, should be prioritized after critical issues.
- **Medium**: Moderate impact, standard resolution timeframes apply.
- **Low**: Minimal impact, can be scheduled into regular maintenance.

## Triage Precision Metric
Assess issues using a triage score based on severity, urgency, and impact. The precision metric helps to determine which issues require immediate focus and which can follow the normal process.

## Turnaround Speed and % Upheld Stats
Guidelines and expected targets:
- **Critical**: Response within 1 hour, resolution within 24 hours, 98% upheld.
- **High**: Response within 4 hours, resolution within 48 hours, 95% upheld.
- **Medium**: Response within 24 hours, resolution within 72 hours, 90% upheld.
- **Low**: Response within 48 hours, resolution within 5 days, 85% upheld.

## Issue-Type Heatmap
The heatmap will visually display areas where AuntEdna provides significant operational improvements. Red for critical improvements, yellow for moderate, and green for minor enhancements.

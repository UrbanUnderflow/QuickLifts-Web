# Partnership Retention Messaging – 40%+ Monthly

## Core Narrative

The easiest way to hit an install target is to buy clicks. The problem is that clicks don’t spot you on your last rep or text you when you don’t show up on Tuesday. Members who arrive through paid channels come in alone, with no shared story about why they’re here or who will notice if they drift. Community-anchored members arrive differently: they sign up because their crew is already inside Pulse, because their coach, their club captain, or their favorite athlete has drawn a clear line between *this season* and *this space*. That difference in origin is why partnership-sourced users behave like regulars instead of passersby.

When someone joins Pulse through a partner gym, they step into a training season that already has a name, a cadence, and familiar faces attached to it. The first time they open the app, they don’t go hunting for value—they see their gym’s logo on the round they’ve been hearing about all week, the coach they know at the top of the leaderboard, and classmates they recognize in the feed. They’re not just "trying an app"; they’re committing to a block their community has already agreed to run together. That shared commitment is what keeps them coming back past day 30, long after the novelty of a new tool wears off.

The same is true for run clubs and brand-led crews. A runner who lands on Pulse because their club captain framed the next six weeks as a "5K build" or "base miles block" isn’t making a solo experiment; they’re opting into a story they’ll talk about at coffee after the Sunday long run. A fan who joins via a brand campaign isn’t just chasing a discount code—they’re signing up to train inside a co-branded challenge their favorite athlete is publicly leading. In both cases, the social cost of disappearing is higher, and the emotional reward of sticking with it is shared, not private.

Paid channels can spike numbers, but they rarely generate this kind of belonging on their own. Partnership channels do, because they meet people where they already feel seen and then offer a concrete way to keep that feeling going between touchpoints. Pulse’s job is to make that through-line obvious: when you come in through your community, you arrive into a named season, a visible cohort, and a rhythm that matches the life you already live with these people. That’s why, month after month, partnership-sourced members behave less like traffic and more like the core of the gym, the club, or the brand’s training story—and why a 40%+ retention line isn’t a miracle, it’s the natural outcome of how they got here.

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Community Over Coupons

**Idea:** Members stay when they feel accountable to people they know, not to promo codes they’ve already used.

- **Retention anchored in relationships.** Users who join through a gym, club, or brand crew are arriving into existing social ties—coaches, captains, and teammates who will notice if they vanish after week two.
- **Lower churn after the first month.** When the initial offer or hype fades, partnership-sourced members still have a reason to open Pulse: the next session their group is doing together, not another discount.
- **Behavior you can see in the feed.** Instead of one-time redemptions, partners see repeat check-ins, familiar names finishing rounds together, and blocks that carry the same faces from start to finish.

### 2. Training That Sticks

**Idea:** People retain when their training has a clear arc and they know where they are inside it; Pulse gives partners the scaffolding to make that true for their community.

- **Named seasons, not endless scroll.** Partnership programs run as defined blocks (e.g., 30-day strength streak, 6-week base miles), so members understand what they’ve signed up for and what "done" looks like.
- **Progress that feels earned.** Checkpoints, streaks, and completion markers give members visible proof they’re moving through a season, making it painful—in a good way—to break the chain.
- **Habits tied to real-life rhythms.** Blocks are designed around the cadence of the partner’s community (class schedules, club nights, drop calendars), so the behavior required to stick is already embedded in members’ weeks.

### 3. Shared Wins You Can See

**Idea:** The more often people see their effort reflected back by their community, the more likely they are to keep showing up past day 30.

- **Cohort views that spotlight stickiness.** Partners can see which cohorts keep logging sessions together after week four, and members can see that they’re not the only ones still in it.
- **Moments worth celebrating publicly.** Co-branded badges, round completions, and streak milestones give gyms, brands, and clubs real achievements to shout out—inside Pulse and on social—without inflating the truth.
- **Signals that inform the next season.** Retention-focused metrics (returning participants per block, blocks completed per member) help partners double down on formats and rituals that people actually stay with, not just sample.

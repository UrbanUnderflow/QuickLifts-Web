# Partnership Playbook Messaging

## Core Narrative

Every strong fitness community already has a home. It might be a corner of the gym where the regulars always end up, a WhatsApp thread that lights up before every run, or a creator’s stories that people watch before they pick up a barbell. Pulse doesn’t try to replace that home; it gives it a digital floorplan. When a partner comes onto Pulse, we don’t ask them to fit into our product. We make the product disappear into their rituals so members feel, instantly, "this is still us"—just with clearer structure and more ways to show up for each other.

For an athletic brand, that means every drop and every campaign stops being a content moment and starts being a training season. Instead of one-and-done posts and screenshots of workouts, Pulse turns the brand’s POV on performance into a tangible, time-bound story: a 30‑day strength streak, a six‑week recovery block, an eight‑week base‑building season. The app gives their athletes and creators a shareable way to lead those seasons, and gives the brand a live view of who is actually moving with them—not just liking a post. The community doesn’t see "a platform integration"; they see their favorite brand finally giving them a place to do the thing they’ve been trying to do in DMs and Notes apps for years.

For community gyms, Pulse becomes the connective tissue between the whiteboard and the rest of the week. Members already trust their coaches, already cheer in class, already feel like this is "their" spot. What they don’t have is a clean way to carry that feeling into the days they’re not in the building. A Pulse partnership lets a gym turn programming blocks and in‑house challenges into followable, co‑branded rounds that live on people’s phones without fragmenting into ten different spreadsheets and group chats. When a member opens Pulse, they don’t see "some app"; they see their gym—its logo, its language, its people—organized into seasons they can commit to.

For run clubs, the product is the ritual: meet spot, warm‑up jokes, that first kilometer where the group settles in, the debrief at coffee. Pulse respects that by treating the app as a frame, not the show. A run club partnership doesn’t add friction; it gives captains a clean way to declare the season (e.g., "5K build", "base miles", "recovery reset"), set weekly Pulse rounds around those intentions, and let members log the story they’re already living—without losing the analog magic of the run itself. Over time, that creates a durable archive of the club’s identity: which blocks people returned for, which distances they grew into, which rituals stuck.

Across brands, gyms, and run clubs, the through‑line is simple: Pulse is where your community’s training story stops living in the cracks. We’re not here to own the relationship; we’re here to give it a container that can handle the scale your next season deserves. A partner who brings their energy, identity, and standards to the table gets a Pulse surface that feels native to them and intuitive to their people—and a path from first conversation to first finished round that fits inside a real campaign timeline, not a nine‑month implementation cycle.

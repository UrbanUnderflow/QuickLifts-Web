# Partner ICP Examples

Short, concrete scenarios for how Pulse would talk about a "Pulse × [Partner] Community" by ICP. These are meant to be drop-in snippets for emails, decks, and landing pages — edit details (names, cities, distances) to fit the actual partner, but keep the structure.

---

## Brands

### 1. Pulse × Tier-One Athletic Brand — Race Build Community

Your race calendar is already full; Pulse turns it into a season your community can actually train inside of. "Pulse × [Brand] Race Build" runs as a 10–12 week challenge block where your athletes and ambassadors lead the plan, your product stories show up in the work (not just the ads), and runners can see their long runs, tempos, and tune-up races stack up together.

**Snippet (deck/email):**  
"Pulse × [Brand] Race Build is where your community trains together for the same finish line — with ambassadors hosting weekly rounds, runners checking in after every key session, and your story woven into the season they’ll remember long after race day."

### 2. Pulse × Performance Apparel Brand — Strength Season Community

You’ve already built the product that supports serious training; Pulse helps your community feel the difference when they use it consistently. "[Brand] Strength Season on Pulse" runs as a six-week strength block where athletes follow a simple, shared plan, log their sessions, and see streaks and PRs build alongside people training in the same gear.

**Snippet (landing page):**  
"Join the [Brand] Strength Season on Pulse — a six-week block led by our athletes, designed to stack small, visible wins in the weight room so you feel the difference every time you pull on your kit."

### 3. Pulse × Recovery / Wellness Brand — Reset Community

Your products already support recovery; Pulse turns that into a rhythm people can feel. "[Brand] Reset on Pulse" is a 21-day challenge focused on sleep, mobility, and low-intensity movement, with simple daily check-ins and prompts that help your community notice how much better training feels when they actually recover.

**Snippet (email):**  
"Pulse × [Brand] Reset is a 21-day recovery season where your community checks in on sleep, mobility, and easy movement — and sees, in real time, how much better their training feels when they give recovery the same respect as the hard sessions."

## Gyms / Studios

### 1. Pulse × CrossFit Box — Block Community

You already run 6–8 week cycles; Pulse makes them a shared story your whole membership can see. "[Gym Name] Strength Block on Pulse" turns your next cycle into a visible challenge where members join with a tap, log their lifts, and see streaks and PRs build across the community — not just on the whiteboard.

**Snippet (deck/email):**  
"With Pulse × [Gym Name], your next 8-week strength block becomes a community challenge — members join with a tap, check in after each session, and see their streaks and PRs stack up alongside the rest of the crew."

### 2. Pulse × Multi-Studio Franchise — Season Community

Across locations, the programming is already aligned; Pulse gives you one challenge layer to tie it together. "[Studio Brand] Summer Season on Pulse" runs as a 6–10 week series of themed challenges (strength, conditioning, recovery) where local coaches host rounds, members check in from any studio, and HQ gets a clear view of how many people are joining, finishing, and coming back.

**Snippet (landing page):**  
"Pulse × [Studio Brand] Summer Season connects every studio in one shared challenge story — local coaches lead the rounds, members check in from any location, and you can see, week by week, how many people are staying with the season."

### 3. Pulse × Cycle or HIIT Studio — Ritual Community

Your best classes are already weekly rituals; Pulse helps those rituals live beyond the room. "[Studio Name] Tuesday Crew on Pulse" becomes a recurring challenge where riders or athletes check in every week, see familiar names, and keep their streak alive — even when they can’t make it into the studio.

**Snippet (email):**  
"Pulse × [Studio Name] Tuesday Crew turns your most-loved class into a season-long ritual — members check in every week, keep their streak alive, and see who else is doing the work with them, whether they’re in-studio or training from home."

## Run Clubs

### 1. Pulse × City Run Club — Season Community

Your calendar already has base blocks, race builds, and off-season crews; Pulse lets your runners see that as one story. "[Club Name] Season on Pulse" maps your weekly runs and key workouts into a clear season view, with captains leading rounds, runners checking in after every session, and the whole crew seeing what they actually did together.

**Snippet (landing page):**  
"Pulse × [Club Name] Season is the digital home for your year — base miles, race builds, and off-season crews all in one place, with captains leading weekly rounds and runners seeing the story of what your club actually did together."

### 2. Pulse × Brand-Affiliated Run Community — Race Build Community

You already sponsor bibs and host shakeout runs; Pulse helps you hold the full build. "[Brand] × [Club Name] Race Build on Pulse" becomes a shared plan where pacers and captains run the block inside Pulse, runners check in after key sessions, and the brand shows up as the space that holds the plan, not just the logo on the singlet.

**Snippet (deck/email):**  
"With Pulse × [Brand] × [Club Name] Race Build, your runners don’t just show up on race day — they train together for weeks in advance, following the same plan, checking in after key sessions, and seeing who else is committed to the finish line." 

### 3. Pulse × Social Run Crew — Everyday Community

Not every crew wants a full race build; some just want a simple, steady rhythm. "[Crew Name] Everyday Miles on Pulse" is an ongoing challenge where members log their easy runs, see streaks, and keep tabs on who’s been out there this week — no pressure, just visible consistency.

**Snippet (email/social):**  
"Pulse × [Crew Name] Everyday Miles is the low-pressure space where your runners log the easy stuff, keep their streak alive, and see who else from the crew has been out this week — even when there’s no race on the calendar."

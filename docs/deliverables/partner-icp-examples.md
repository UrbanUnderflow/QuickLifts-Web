# Partner ICP Examples – Partnership-Led Community Growth

Short example scenarios and copy snippets for how we talk about "Pulse × [Partner] Community" across three lanes. Use these as seeds for emails, decks, or landing pages; adjust details (names, timelines, distances) to the specific partner.

---

## Brands

**1. Performance apparel brand – Pulse × [Brand] Strength Season**  
Scenario: A performance apparel brand wants to turn a new strength-focused collection into something their community can train through together.  
Snippet: "Pulse turns [Brand]’s next strength drop into a 6-week season your community can actually move through together. Your athletes host the block, customers join with a tap from your channels, and you see exactly how many people didn’t just see the campaign — they trained with you through every week of it."

**2. Footwear brand – Pulse × [Brand] Race Build Community**  
Scenario: A footwear brand wants to support their community through a 10K or half-marathon cycle around a marquee race.  
Snippet: "With Pulse, [Brand] can give runners more than a race-day moment. Captains host a 6–8 week build inside a Pulse challenge, runners join straight from your email or social, and each week you can see who’s logging miles, who’s on a streak, and who’s moving through the story with your shoes on their feet."

**3. Recovery or wellness brand – Pulse × [Brand] Reset Community**  
Scenario: A recovery/wellness brand wants to offer a co-branded reset that’s more than a one-off email.  
Snippet: "Pulse turns [Brand]’s ‘reset’ into a real-life recovery challenge your community can share. Ambassadors host a 21-day block with light strength, walks, and mobility built around your products, and members can show up in small ways every week — so the next time you talk about recovery, it’s anchored in a season they actually lived."

## Gyms / Studios

**1. Creator-led strength gym – Pulse × [Gym] Block Community**  
Scenario: A single-location strength gym wants to give its 8-week squat cycle a shared story and a simple way to track who’s still in.  
Snippet: "Pulse turns [Gym]’s next 8-week squat cycle into a story the whole gym can step into. Members scan a code in class, join the block with a tap, and check off each week’s sessions together — giving your coaches a live feel for who’s still in the crew and who might need a nudge before they drift."

**2. Multi-location studio – Pulse × [Studio] Season Community**  
Scenario: A multi-city studio wants everyone to feel like they’re in the same season, no matter which location they attend.  
Snippet: "With Pulse, [Studio] can run one shared challenge season across every location. Local coaches host the blocks, members in Brooklyn and Austin join the same 6-week series, and you can see how many riders or clients ride out the full season instead of disappearing after week two."

**3. Performance-focused facility – Pulse × [Gym] Performance Community**  
Scenario: A performance gym (CrossFit, performance lab) wants to keep athletes connected between classes and phases.  
Snippet: "Pulse gives [Gym] a digital spine for the community that already shows up hard in person. Coaches can drop each training phase into a challenge, athletes check in on and off the floor, and streaks make it obvious who’s still moving through the block so you can celebrate them before they become another silent churn."

## Run Clubs

**1. City run club – Pulse × [Club] Season Community**  
Scenario: A city run club wants to turn their weekly runs into a real season leading into a target race.  
Snippet: "Pulse turns [Club]’s Tuesday tempos and Saturday long runs into a 6-week season your crew can actually see. Captains host the block, runners join straight from the group chat, and every week you know who’s logging miles, who’s on a streak, and who might need a check-in before race day."

**2. Brand-affiliated run crew – Pulse × [Brand] Run Community**  
Scenario: A brand-backed run crew wants to give members something to move through together between tentpole events.  
Snippet: "With Pulse, [Brand]’s run community doesn’t have to wait for the next race photo to feel connected. You can host a co-branded base miles season or race build where pacing plans, routes, and product moments live in one shared challenge — so every week, your runners can show they’re still in the story with you."

**3. Campus or grassroots crew – Pulse × [Crew] Everyday Community**  
Scenario: A university or neighborhood crew wants to keep people engaged through busy seasons and breaks.  
Snippet: "Pulse helps [Crew] hold onto its energy even when schedules get messy. Captains can host simple weekly blocks — two runs plus one ‘do something that moves you’ day — and give people a way to show they’re still in the crew’s story, even when they can’t make every usual meetup."

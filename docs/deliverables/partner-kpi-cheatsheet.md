# Partner KPI Cheatsheet – Partnership-Led Community Growth

Use these KPIs when framing Pulse’s impact to partners. They’re all behavior metrics: they describe what people actually do together, not just what they click.

---

## 1. Challenge Join Rate

**What it is:** The share of a partner’s reachable community (email list, app users, social followers, members) that joins a given Pulse challenge or block.  
**How Pulse moves it:** By letting creators, coaches, and captains launch challenges directly from the channels people already use, with one-tap joins and clear, human framing (e.g., "6-week strength season"), Pulse makes it much easier for more people to say "yes" to a shared journey — not just passively see a post.

## 2. Challenge Completion Rate

**What it is:** The percentage of participants who actually finish a challenge or block (reach the end date with enough check-ins to count as completed).  
**How Pulse moves it:** Pulse keeps people in the story with visible streaks, soft accountability from leaders, and small, specific actions instead of vague goals. That makes it much more likely that someone who joins will still be there at week four or six, which partners can show in decks as completed journeys, not abandoned campaigns.

## 3. Repeat-Challenge Rate

**What it is:** The percentage of challenge finishers who roll straight into the next Pulse-powered block with the same partner (or ecosystem).  
**How Pulse moves it:** Because blocks are sequenced — reset weeks, build cycles, race seasons — there is always a clear "next thing" to do together. That turns one-off campaigns into ongoing seasons, and gives partners a clean number for how many people keep coming back for the next chapter.

## 4. Active Creators / Hosts

**What it is:** The number of athletes, coaches, captains, or staff actively running Pulse challenges for a partner in a given period.  
**How Pulse moves it:** Pulse makes it simple for more leaders to host blocks — with templates, scheduling, and built-in check-in flows — so partners can activate a broader slice of their roster instead of relying on one or two faces. Higher active-host counts usually correlate with more resilient, distributed community engagement.

## 5. Streak Weeks

**What it is:** The count (or percentage) of weeks where a meaningful slice of the community maintains a shared streak inside a challenge (for example, hitting 3+ touchpoints per week).  
**How Pulse moves it:** By structuring challenges around weekly rituals — Monday lifts, Tuesday tempos, Saturday long runs, reset days — and surfacing streaks visibly, Pulse stacks more "alive" weeks where people are doing something together on purpose.

## 6. Inactivity Gap Rate

**What it is:** The percentage of active community members who experience a 4+ day gap with no Pulse-tracked participation during a block or season.  
**How Pulse moves it:** Pulse spots early drop-offs and gives leaders tools to respond — nudges, shout-outs, light "catch-up" tasks — so gaps shrink. For partners, a lower inactivity gap rate means fewer people drifting away mid-campaign or mid-cycle.

## 7. Creator-Led Participation Rate

**What it is:** The share of the partner’s reachable community that joins at least one creator- or coach-led Pulse block in a given season.  
**How Pulse moves it:** Because challenges are built around the people a community already trusts, Pulse makes it easy for more followers or members to cross the line from "I watch" to "I’m in." That’s a story partners can tell internally: a measurable percentage of their community is now inside structured, socially visible journeys led by their own people.

# Partner Messaging Guide – Partnership-Led Community Growth

This guide captures how we talk to community-driven brands and spaces about Pulse as their digital home. It’s written to be copy-safe: you can lift lines into decks, landing pages, and outreach emails without falling into generic fitness wallpaper.

---

## Core Narrative

Pulse is the challenge layer that turns your existing audience into an active community moving through seasons together. Instead of asking your people to screenshot workouts, hunt through feeds, or remember another promo code, Pulse gives them one place to join clear, time-bound challenges with the creators, coaches, and captains they already trust.

For partners, that means your campaigns, training blocks, and race seasons stop living as marketing moments and start living as shared journeys. A Gymshark drop becomes a 30-day strength season. A CrossFit gym’s squat cycle becomes an 8-week crew moving through the same milestones. A run club’s calendar becomes a race-ready build where captains can see who’s still in the story and who needs a nudge.

Partnership-Led Community Growth is simple: Pulse only wins when your community does. We plug into the channels you already own — social, email, apps, gym floors, club chats — and give you a single challenge layer that keeps people showing up together and makes the health of that movement measurable for both sides.

---

## Messaging Pillars

Use these pillars as the spine for partner decks, emails, and in-app copy. Everything we say to partners should hang off one or more of these ideas.

### 1. Creator-Led, Community-First

Pulse is built around the people your community already shows up for — athletes, coaches, captains, and studio owners. They host the challenges; we handle the structure. When we talk to partners, we emphasize that Pulse doesn’t replace their people — it gives those leaders a real home for the seasons they’re already trying to run.

**How to say it:**
- "Pulse turns your athletes and coaches into challenge hosts, not just faces in a feed."
- "Your community shows up for people, not products — we give those people a place to run real seasons together."

### 2. Communities as the Product

On Pulse, the real product is the feeling of moving through a story together, not a list of app features. Partnership messaging should frame Pulse as a way to deepen the community experience your brand or space already offers, not as "yet another tool" to manage.

**How to say it:**
- "We’re not selling an app; we’re giving your community a story to move through together."
- "Success isn’t clicks — it’s how many of your people show up for week four of the block."

### 3. Co-Branded Rituals, Not One-Off Ads

Every partnership touchpoint should feel like a ritual the community looks forward to — Monday strength crews, Saturday long runs, reset weeks between heavy cycles. Messaging should highlight recurring, co-branded challenges that members can build habits around, not just one-shot campaigns that spike and disappear.

**How to say it:**
- "We turn your campaigns into rituals your community comes back for every season."
- "Instead of a one-week promo, you get a four-week block that people finish together — and ask to do again."

### 4. One Challenge Layer Across All Your Surfaces

Pulse sits on top of the channels partners already use — Instagram, email, in-app surfaces, gym TVs, club chats — so they don’t have to rebuild their stack. Messaging should make it clear that we integrate into their existing ecosystem and bring coherence to it, rather than asking them to move their community somewhere new.

**How to say it:**
- "Pulse plugs into the channels you already own and gives you one challenge layer across all of them."
- "Your community stays where it is — Pulse just connects the dots between your posts, your classes, and your events."

### 5. Shared, Community-Real KPIs

We should always bring the conversation back to a small set of behavior metrics that matter to both Pulse and the partner: challenge joins, completion rate, repeat-challenge rate, streak weeks, active creators. Messaging should connect partnership outcomes to these KPIs rather than vanity metrics.

**How to say it:**
- "We measure how many of your people joined, how many finished, and how many rolled into the next block."
- "Our job is to shrink inactivity gaps and stack more weeks where your community is doing something together on purpose."

## Voice & Tone

This section defines how we should sound when we talk to partners about community growth on Pulse. It’s a filter, not a script.

### 1. Human, Direct, and Specific

We speak like a smart coach or creator, not a corporate brochure. Avoid jargon and generic claims. Always anchor examples in real behaviors: 30-day streaks, 8-week blocks, Tuesday tempos, Saturday long runs, reset weeks.

**Do:**
- "We’ll turn your next 8-week squat cycle into a shared block that every member can join with a tap."

**Don’t:**
- "We empower your users to unlock their full fitness potential."

### 2. Respect the Partner’s Community

We never talk down to a partner’s members or imply they aren’t already doing meaningful work. Our role is to make their existing community rhythm more visible, more structured, and easier to sustain — not to "fix" it.

**Do:**
- "Your community is already showing up — Pulse gives them a clearer path to move through together."

**Don’t:**
- "Your audience is unengaged; our app will finally motivate them."

### 3. Values-Forward Without Preaching

We care about sustainable movement, credible coaching, and communities that feel good to be part of. Our tone should reflect that without lecturing. When we talk about growth, we tie it back to community health, not just revenue or reach.

**Do:**
- "We’d rather help you build a smaller, committed crew that sticks with you for three seasons than chase a big spike that disappears in a week."

**Don’t:**
- "Our growth hacks will explode your engagement overnight."

### 4. Confident, But Not Desperate

Pulse is selective about partnerships. Our messaging should feel like an invitation to do something serious together, not a cold pitch begging for attention. We are clear about what we do best — challenge infrastructure for real communities — and honest about what we don’t do.

**Do:**
- "If you’re looking for a way to turn this campaign into a challenge your community can actually move through together, we should talk."

**Don’t:**
- "We’ll do anything to get your logo in our app."

### 5. Align on Story and Metrics

Every conversation should end with a shared understanding of the story we’re telling the community and the metrics we’ll watch together. Our tone should make it easy for partners to repeat that story internally, and confident enough that we’re willing to be held to the numbers.

**Do:**
- "Internally, you can frame this as: ‘We’re running a four-week co-branded challenge where our coaches lead, and we’ll track joins, completions, and who signs up for the next block.’"

**Don’t:**
- "Just trust us, it’ll feel more engaging."

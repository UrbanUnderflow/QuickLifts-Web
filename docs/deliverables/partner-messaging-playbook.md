# Partner Messaging Playbook

This playbook is for anyone talking about Pulse to partners — brands, gyms/studios, and run clubs. It translates our Partnership-Led Community Growth north star into concrete language you can use in decks, emails, and product surfaces.

## Core Narrative

Pulse is the digital home for serious fitness communities — the place where brands, gyms, and run clubs host the challenge seasons their people can actually finish together. Our partners already have the hard part: a community that shows up for them online and in person. Pulse gives that community one shared challenge layer where they can move through blocks, seasons, and rituals together instead of getting scattered across PDFs, DMs, and spreadsheets.

In practice, that looks like 6–8 week strength cycles, 21-day recovery resets, base miles seasons, and race builds hosted by the athletes, coaches, and captains a community already trusts. Members join with a tap from the channels they already use, check off each session, see their own streaks and their friends’ progress, and always know what the next shared step is. Pulse holds the story of those seasons end to end — who joined, who stayed, and how many came back for the next chapter.

When we talk to partners, we’re not selling an app download. We’re inviting them to plug their community into a structure that makes their culture visible and measurable. Partnership-Led Community Growth means that when a partner’s community gets stronger and more connected on Pulse, we grow with them — through better retention, richer stories, and more people choosing to train inside their world.

## Messaging Pillars

1. **Creator-Led Challenges**  
   We lead with the people, not the product. Pulse is built around athletes, coaches, and captains who already carry trust in a community and gives them tools to host real challenge seasons — not just post one-off programs. When we talk about features, we frame them as ways to make it easier for those leaders to bring people into a shared story.

2. **Communities as the Product**  
   The most valuable thing our partners have is not content or inventory — it’s the feeling of belonging their community already shares. Messaging should emphasize that Pulse deepens that feeling by turning it into a sequence of shared blocks and rituals, not by replacing it with a new feed or algorithm.

3. **Co-Branded Rituals, Not Ads**  
   Every Pulse partnership should feel like a recurring ritual the community looks forward to — a Monday strength crew, a Saturday long run, a reset week — with the partner woven into the experience. We avoid language about "impressions" and "exposure" and instead talk about co-branded seasons that members finish together and want to repeat.

4. **One Challenge Layer Across Existing Channels**  
   Partners don’t need another platform to maintain. Pulse sits on top of the channels they already own — social, email, apps, gym floors, group chats — and provides a single challenge layer that keeps the story consistent. Our messaging should make it clear that Pulse respects the partner’s existing ecosystem and plugs into it instead of competing with it.

5. **Shared, Behavior-First KPIs**  
   We measure what people actually do together: challenge joins, completion rate, repeat-challenge rate, streak weeks, active creators. When we talk about results, we use these behavior metrics and show how Pulse moves them over time, so partners can retell the story internally without translating dashboards or chasing vanity numbers.

## Voice & Tone

1. **Plain, grounded language**  
   We speak like a thoughtful coach, not a growth deck. Use concrete phrases ("6-week strength season," "Tuesday tempos and Saturday long runs") instead of abstractions ("optimize engagement"). If a sentence could fit on any generic fitness app site, make it more specific.

2. **Respect for partner communities**  
   Our partners and their members are sharp, experienced, and often already training hard together. Avoid anything that sounds like explaining fitness 101. Assume they know their world deeply; our role is to offer a better structure for the community they’ve already built.

3. **Values-forward, not preachy**  
   We care about community, trust, and long-term growth, but we don’t lecture about it. Let the structure of Pulse — challenges, seasons, shared rituals — show our values in action. When we name values, do it simply ("we care about who comes back for the next season, not just who clicks once").

4. **Partner as co-creator, not customer**  
   Talk to partners as collaborators building something with us, not buyers of a toolkit. Use "we" and "together" when describing what happens on Pulse ("together we can turn your next drop into a 30-day season your community can actually finish"). Make it clear we are protecting their brand and culture, not trying to overwrite it.

5. **Honest about what we can and can’t promise**  
   We don’t guarantee follower spikes or overnight revenue jumps. We do promise a cleaner way to see who joined a season, who stayed, and who came back. When in doubt, bring the conversation back to behavior metrics and real examples of how communities move through challenges on Pulse.

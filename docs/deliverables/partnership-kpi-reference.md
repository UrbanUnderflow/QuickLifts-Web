# Partnership KPI Reference – Partnership-Led Community Growth

Use these KPIs when framing Pulse’s impact with partners. They are all behavior metrics — what people actually do together — and each one should be backed by real challenge data, not vanity numbers.

---

## 1. Challenge Join Rate

**What it is:** The share of a partner’s reachable community (email list, app users, social followers, members) that joins a given Pulse challenge or block.  
**Why it matters:** A high join rate shows that a partner’s community is willing to step into a shared journey, not just passively watch content. Pulse moves this by letting creators, coaches, and captains launch challenges directly from the channels people already use, with one-tap joins and clear, human framing (e.g., "6-week strength season").

## 2. Challenge Completion Rate

**What it is:** The percentage of participants who actually finish a challenge or block — reaching the end date with enough check-ins to count as completed.  
**Why it matters:** Completion shows that a campaign held people’s attention long enough to become a real season in their life. Pulse improves completion with visible streaks, soft accountability from leaders, and specific, doable actions instead of vague goals, so more people who join are still there at weeks four, six, or eight.

## 3. Repeat-Challenge Rate

**What it is:** The percentage of challenge finishers who roll straight into the next Pulse-powered block with the same partner (or ecosystem).  
**Why it matters:** Repeat-challenge rate turns one-off campaigns into ongoing seasons. Pulse boosts this by sequencing blocks — reset weeks, build cycles, race seasons — so there’s always a clear "next thing" to do together, and partners can point to how many people choose to stay in the story.

## 4. Active Creators / Hosts

**What it is:** The number of athletes, coaches, captains, or staff actively running Pulse challenges for a partner in a given period.  
**Why it matters:** Distributed leadership makes communities more resilient. Pulse makes it simple for more leaders to host blocks — with templates, scheduling, and built-in check-in flows — so partners can activate more of their roster instead of relying on one or two faces, typically correlating with deeper and more durable engagement.

## 5. Streak Weeks

**What it is:** The count (or percentage) of weeks where a meaningful slice of the community maintains a shared streak inside a challenge (for example, hitting 3+ touchpoints per week).  
**Why it matters:** Streak weeks are weeks when the community feels "alive" — people are showing up on purpose, together. Pulse structures challenges around weekly rituals (Monday lifts, Tuesday tempos, Saturday long runs, reset days) and surfaces streaks visibly, creating more of these high-energy weeks partners can point to.

## 6. Inactivity Gap Rate

**What it is:** The percentage of active community members who experience a 4+ day gap with no Pulse-tracked participation during a block or season.  
**Why it matters:** Long gaps are where people quietly drop out of a campaign. Pulse spots early drop-offs and gives leaders tools to respond — nudges, shout-outs, light "catch-up" tasks — so gaps shrink. For partners, a lower inactivity gap rate means fewer members drifting away mid-season.

## 7. Creator-Led Participation Rate

**What it is:** The share of a partner’s reachable community that joins at least one creator- or coach-led Pulse block in a given season.  
**Why it matters:** This metric shows how many people have crossed the line from "I watch" to "I’m in." Because Pulse builds challenges around leaders a community already trusts, partners can grow this number over time and tell a simple story internally: a measurable percentage of our people are now inside structured, socially visible journeys led by our own athletes, coaches, and captains.

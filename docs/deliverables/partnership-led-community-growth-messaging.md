# Partnership-Led Community Growth

Pulse’s north star is Partnership-Led Community Growth: we grow by becoming the fitness platform that the most energized, community-driven brands, gyms, and run clubs choose as their digital home. For partners, that means Pulse isn’t a side campaign or another app in the stack — it’s the shared challenge layer where their athletes, coaches, and captains run real seasons their people can finish together, and where growth is measured in how many of those people keep coming back for the next chapter.

## Core Narrative

Pulse is where serious fitness communities go when they’re ready for more than a hashtag challenge and a landing page. Your creators, coaches, and captains already have people who show up for them; Pulse gives those people one home for the creator-led blocks, seasons, and rituals that keep them coming back. Instead of a program link that disappears in DMs, they see a living challenge with a clear start and finish, a simple way to check in, and a live view of who else is in it with them.

For creators, that looks like strength blocks, reset seasons, or race builds their followers can join with a tap — and stay with because they can see their own streaks and their friends’ progress. For gyms and studios, it’s 6–8 week cycles and weekly rituals that extend the energy of the class floor into the rest of the week, with coach-led challenges that turn isolated sessions into a shared story across the whole membership. For brands, it’s product drops and campaign themes that become co-branded seasons — 21-day recovery resets, base miles cycles, performance blocks — hosted by the athletes and ambassadors their community already trusts.

Across all three lanes, Pulse keeps communities showing up together by making the next shared step always obvious. Blocks don’t end in a void; they roll into the next season. Members aren’t left guessing what to do on their own; they can see what their people are doing together right now. That’s what Partnership-Led Community Growth means in practice: every time a partner launches a Pulse challenge, they’re not just driving clicks — they’re building a measurable, repeatable rhythm their community can feel.

## Messaging Pillars

- **Creator-Led Challenges**  
  We always start with the people, not the product. Pulse is built around athletes, coaches, and captains who already carry trust in a community and gives them tools to host real challenge seasons instead of one-off posts. Partnership-led growth shows up here as more of your community choosing to follow your own leaders through shared journeys on Pulse.

- **Communities as the Product**  
  The most valuable thing our partners have is not content or inventory — it’s the feeling of belonging their community already shares. Pulse strengthens that by turning it into a sequence of shared blocks and rituals, so partnership growth comes from deepening an existing culture, not bolting on a new feed or promo channel.

- **Co-Branded Rituals, Not Ads**  
  A Pulse partnership should feel like a recurring ritual your community looks forward to — a Monday strength crew, a Saturday long run, a reset week — with your brand woven into the experience. We measure success not in impressions, but in how many people finish those seasons and come back for the next one, which is the essence of partnership-led community growth.

- **One Challenge Layer Across Existing Channels**  
  Pulse plugs into the channels you already own — social, email, apps, gym floors, group chats — and provides a single challenge layer that keeps the story consistent across all of them. That’s how we help partners scale community seasons without scattering members across disconnected tools or losing the thread of the story.

- **Shared, Behavior-First KPIs**  
  We anchor every partnership in behavior metrics like challenge join rate, completion rate, repeat-challenge rate, streak weeks, and active creators. These are numbers partners can take into internal conversations to prove that their community is moving together on Pulse — a clear signal that Partnership-Led Community Growth is working in practice, not just on a slide.

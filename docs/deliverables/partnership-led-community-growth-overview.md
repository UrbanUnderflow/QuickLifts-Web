# Partnership-Led Community Growth

Pulse’s north star is Partnership-Led Community Growth: we become the fitness platform that the most energized, community-driven brands and spaces choose as their digital home. Instead of chasing cold installs, we grow by helping partners deepen the communities they already have — and by giving those communities a real place to train through shared seasons together.

In practice, that means three primary lanes. For **brands**, Pulse is the challenge layer that turns product drops and campaign themes into co-branded seasons — 21-day recovery resets, base miles cycles, performance blocks — hosted by the athletes and ambassadors their community already trusts. For **gyms and studios**, Pulse is the digital spine behind 6–8 week cycles and weekly rituals, where members scan a code in class, join with a tap, and stay connected to coaches and each other between sessions. For **run clubs**, Pulse turns Tuesday tempos and Saturday long runs into actual seasons, with captains hosting blocks that runners can join straight from the channels they already live in.

Across all three lanes, being the digital home means Pulse isn’t trying to replace a partner’s world — we sit underneath it. Social feeds, email lists, group chats, and gym floors stay where they are; Pulse links them together with one shared challenge layer. That layer is where partners can see who joined, who stayed, who came back for the next chapter, and where community growth is measured in streaks, completions, and repeat seasons rather than clicks. That is what Partnership-Led Community Growth looks like when it’s real.

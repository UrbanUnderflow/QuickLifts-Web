# Publish a daily Macra KPI snapshot from Scoreboard, Experiments, purchase logs, cancel reasons, retargeting, and AppsFlyer coverage

## Core narrative

The daily Macra KPI snapshot must be assembled from the existing Scoreboard, Experiments, purchase logs, cancel reasons, retargeting state, and AppsFlyer coverage before any partner-facing interpretation is shared. That means the operating view is built from the real source inputs first, then any external summary, partner update, or funnel narrative is written from that assembled snapshot rather than from stale takeaways, isolated metrics, or unsupported assumptions.

## Messaging pillars

### 1. Scoreboard and Experiments establish the daily operating truth

The top line of the daily snapshot starts with **Scoreboard** and **Experiments** because they define what moved, what held, and what changed since the last read. Any partner-facing interpretation has to begin there so the story reflects the current operating truth rather than a recycled narrative from a stronger or weaker day.

### 2. Purchase logs and cancel reasons explain the quality behind the count

The daily snapshot is not just volume reporting. **Purchase logs** show whether the funnel is turning intent into paid behavior, while **cancel reasons** show where trust, clarity, or expectation may be breaking after the start. Those two sources keep the KPI view grounded in signal quality, not just movement.

### 3. Retargeting state and AppsFlyer coverage keep attribution and follow-up honest

**Retargeting state** shows whether follow-up is active, stale, or mismatched to the user moment, and **AppsFlyer coverage** helps show whether the day’s results are broad-based or concentrated in a narrower traffic mix. Together, they keep the snapshot honest about what was influenced by messaging, what was influenced by acquisition mix, and what still needs cleaner attribution before it becomes a partner claim.

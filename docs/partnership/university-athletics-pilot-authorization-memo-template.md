# University Athletics Pilot Authorization Memo

**Purpose**

This memo records the Athletics Department's permission for a limited, voluntary PulseCheck pilot operated by Pulse Intelligence Labs, Inc. ("PulseCheck") in partnership with AuntEdna. This memo is intended for a pre-contract pilot period and is not a substitute for a full master services agreement, data-sharing agreement, business associate agreement, or university-wide contract if those later become necessary.

**Institution**

- University: `[University Name]`
- Athletics Department: `[Athletics Department Name]`
- Team / Program: `[Team / Program Name]`
- Pilot Dates: `[Start Date]` to `[End Date]`

**Pilot Summary**

The Athletics Department authorizes PulseCheck to offer a limited, voluntary pilot to eligible student-athletes affiliated with the team or program listed above. Participation is opt-in only. No student-athlete is required to participate as a condition of team membership, scholarship status, roster status, care access, or receipt of athletics benefits.

PulseCheck and AuntEdna may support pilot workflows that include check-ins, readiness or recovery signals, session activity, survey responses, escalation or support workflow events, and connected wearable or health data the participant affirmatively authorizes. Some parts of the pilot may be incomplete, experimental, or subject to change during the pilot period.

**Department Authorization**

The Athletics Department confirms the following:

1. The department authorizes PulseCheck to invite eligible student-athletes in the identified team or program to review disclosures and voluntarily opt into the pilot.
2. The department authorizes the identified department staff to coordinate pilot logistics, distribute invites, and support athlete questions about enrollment and participation.
3. The department understands that AuntEdna is participating as an approved pilot partner for support, escalation, and related workflow operations as described in the participant disclosures and pilot materials.
4. The department understands that this memo authorizes a limited pilot only and does not obligate the university or department to purchase services, expand the pilot, or enter a longer-term relationship.

**Data and Records Boundary**

The parties intend this pilot to follow a minimum-necessary approach. Participant-facing disclosures and invite materials should describe what categories of information may be collected, used, or disclosed, for what purpose, and to whom.

If education records, school-linked data, or athletics-program records are involved, the department confirms that any disclosure will be handled only to the extent permitted by applicable law and institutional policy, including use of participant consent where required. The pilot materials should identify:

- the categories of records or information involved,
- the purpose of the disclosure or use, and
- the person or class of persons or organizations that may receive the information, including PulseCheck, AuntEdna, authorized athletics staff, sports medicine staff, and approved support partners participating in the pilot workflow.

This memo does not authorize unrestricted access to student records, medical records, or protected health information.

**Emergency and Care Boundary**

The Athletics Department understands and agrees that PulseCheck is not emergency response, not crisis care, and not a substitute for medical care, mental health treatment, or emergency services. AuntEdna may participate in support or escalation workflows where configured, but this memo does not replace university emergency procedures, mandated reporting duties, campus safety procedures, clinician judgment, or licensed care obligations.

The Athletics Department remains responsible for its own institutional emergency, reporting, and student-support procedures unless and until a later written agreement states otherwise.

**Pilot Operations**

During the pilot period:

- PulseCheck may provide participant-facing onboarding, disclosures, and pilot notices.
- PulseCheck and AuntEdna may operate the pilot workflow described in the participant materials.
- The Athletics Department may identify staff points of contact for coordination, follow-up, and participant questions.
- The parties may review aggregated or de-identified pilot feedback and operational learnings to decide whether a longer-term arrangement should be considered.

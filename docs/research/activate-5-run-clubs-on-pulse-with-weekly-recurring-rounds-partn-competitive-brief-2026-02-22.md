# How are leading run clubs structuring digital community + weekly runs today?

## Examples

### 1. Nike Run Club (Global)
- **Location / scope:** Global, with city-based run crews in major metros (e.g., NYC, London, LA).
- **How they organize runs digitally:** Primarily through the Nike Run Club (NRC) mobile app and localized event pages; city crews often maintain Instagram accounts for announcements and sign-ups.
- **Main community hub / events URL:** https://www.nike.com/nrc-app
- **Tooling stack (Unverified):** NRC mobile app for run tracking and programming, Nike membership accounts for authentication, plus Instagram for local crew communication and storytelling (e.g., city accounts linking back to NRC events). Source: https://www.nike.com/nrc-app
- **Attendance / accountability (Unverified):** App-based event registration and run logs; badges/challenges inside NRC incentivize repeat participation rather than manual attendance checks. Source: https://www.nike.com/nrc-app
- **Social content from runs (Unverified):** Heavy emphasis on Instagram and user-generated content using NRC-branded hashtags; recap posts and stories highlight group runs rather than surfacing in-app social feeds. Source: https://www.nike.com/nrc-app

### 2. November Project (Multi-city, North America & Europe)
- **Location / scope:** Multi-city grassroots fitness and running community with chapters across the U.S., Canada, and select international cities.
- **How they organize runs digitally:** Central website with city-specific pages that list weekly workouts; many chapters also use Facebook/Instagram for event reminders and photos.
- **Main community hub / events URL:** https://november-project.com/
- **Tooling stack (Unverified):** Central WordPress-style site for chapter listings and schedules, plus chapter-level Facebook pages and Instagram accounts for announcements and RSVP details. Source: https://november-project.com/
- **Attendance / accountability (Unverified):** Largely attendance-by-showing-up; some cities may use FB event RSVPs or photo tags to track regulars, but the culture leans toward open, no-signup workouts. Source: https://november-project.com/
- **Social content from runs (Unverified):** Strong culture of posting workout photos, “we showed up” group shots, and city-specific hashtags on Instagram and Facebook; content lives on social platforms rather than a centralized app feed. Source: https://november-project.com/

### 3. Black Girls RUN! (United States)
- **Location / scope:** National U.S. network of local run groups focused on Black women, with chapters in dozens of cities.
- **How they organize runs digitally:** Central website with chapter directory; many local groups coordinate weekly runs via Facebook Groups and email lists linked from the main site.
- **Main community hub / events URL:** https://blackgirlsrun.com/

### 4. Midnight Runners (Global Urban Run Club)
- **Location / scope:** Urban run club with hubs in major cities including London, Berlin, Boston, and others.
- **How they organize runs digitally:** Official website listing city hubs and weekly “bootcamp run” events; city-specific Instagram accounts used to announce runs and share recap content.
- **Main community hub / events URL:** https://www.midnightrunners.com/

### 5. GoodGym (UK)
- **Location / scope:** UK-based running community that combines group runs with community projects across multiple cities.
- **How they organize runs digitally:** Central platform where each city has its own page with recurring “group run” events, sign-up, and attendance tracking.
- **Main community hub / events URL:** https://www.goodgym.org/

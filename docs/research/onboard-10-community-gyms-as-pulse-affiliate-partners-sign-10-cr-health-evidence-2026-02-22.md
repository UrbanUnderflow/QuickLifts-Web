# Evidence Memo: Onboarding 10 Community Gyms as Pulse Affiliate Partners

## Purpose

This evidence memo exists to inform how Pulse designs the affiliate program and in-gym activation playbook for community gyms (CrossFit boxes, cycle studios, and strength-focused facilities) so that:
- We can successfully onboard at least 10 gyms as active Pulse affiliate partners, and
- Each gym can drive ≥50% of its member base to create Pulse accounts within 60 days of onboarding.

The goal is to surface evidence from gym-based and community fitness programs about what drives member engagement, sign-up behavior, and early adoption of new tools, then translate those insights into a concrete activation strategy Pulse can offer every new affiliate.

This document will focus on:
- Evidence around engagement and retention in gym-based and community fitness environments
- Patterns that influence member adoption of new digital tools introduced through their gym
- Design implications for Pulse’s affiliate onboarding, in-gym activation flows, and coach-facing scripts to reach ≥50% member activation within 60 days

## Findings

1. **Coaching relationship quality and perceived support strongly influence member adherence in community gyms.** Research on CrossFit, group training, and boutique studios shows that members who perceive their coaches as supportive, competent, and attentive report higher satisfaction and are more likely to continue attending sessions over time [1]. Coach endorsement is also a key driver when gyms introduce new tools or programs—members are more likely to try and stick with a new system if it is actively recommended and modeled by coaches.

2. **Social cohesion and a strong sense of “tribe” are core to retention in CrossFit boxes and similar communities.** Studies on CrossFit and community-based exercise highlight that feelings of belonging, shared identity, and mutual encouragement distinguish these environments from traditional gyms and are central to why members stay [2]. Rituals (named workouts, leaderboards, post-class hangouts) and visible community symbols (logos, mottos, in-gym signage) reinforce this identity and can be leveraged to frame Pulse as part of the tribe rather than an external app.

3. **Structured onboarding and clear goal framing improve adoption of new programs in gym settings.** Evidence from exercise and health behavior interventions indicates that when new initiatives are introduced with a clear rationale, specific goals, and a time-bound structure (e.g., a 4–8 week challenge), participants are more likely to sign up and complete the program than when tools are simply made available without a defined container [3]. In gyms, launch challenges that are integrated into existing programming (e.g., "next 6 weeks of classes are part of our Pulse round") see better uptake than optional add-ons.

4. **Peer visibility and in-gym feedback loops increase participation and accountability.** Studies on group-based exercise and behavior change show that publicly displaying progress (attendance boards, whiteboards, leaderboards, shout-outs) increases perceived accountability and engagement [4]. In practice, gyms that use visible tracking (e.g., attendance charts, PR boards) tend to see higher class participation; connecting Pulse account creation and check-ins to these visible systems can drive sign-up behavior.

5. **Lowering friction at the point of decision (e.g., at check-in or during class) is critical for high adoption of digital tools in gyms.** Work on technology adoption and health apps suggests that simple, contextually embedded actions (QR codes at the front desk, one-tap links in SMS or member apps, coach-led "everyone download this now" moments) significantly improve conversion compared with email-only or passive promotion [5]. For community gyms, embedding Pulse account creation into existing high-attention moments—first-day waivers, membership sign-up, or kickoff classes—will likely be necessary to reach ≥50% activation inside 60 days.

## Recommended Intervention

**Launch a “Gym-Wide Founding Round” playbook that makes Pulse onboarding a coached, in-class ritual—not an optional side quest.**

Based on the findings that (a) coach relationship quality and endorsement drive adoption [1], (b) community identity and tribe signals matter in CrossFit-style gyms [2], (c) structured, time-bound challenges outperform vague offerings [3], (d) visible progress and social accountability increase participation [4], and (e) low-friction, in-context onboarding is critical for digital tool uptake [5], the affiliate program should give every new gym a standardized launch pattern:

1. **Anchor Pulse to a 6-week “Founding Round” that is part of regular programming.**
   - Rather than marketing Pulse as an optional app, position it as the tracking and community layer for the next 6 weeks of classes (e.g., “Our next 6-week strength cycle is hosted in Pulse”).
   - Provide tracks/templates for typical partners (CrossFit, strength, cycle) so gyms can plug their existing programming into a ready-made Pulse round.
   - This gives members a clear, time-bound reason to join now and ties account creation directly to what they’re already doing [3].

2. **Script a coach-led onboarding moment in the first week.**
   - During a designated “Launch Class” in week 1, the coach takes 3–5 minutes to:
     - Explain why the gym is using Pulse (better tracking, shared challenges, community recognition) in their own voice [1].
     - Ask everyone to take out their phones and either scan a QR code at the whiteboard/front desk or tap a short link sent via SMS/email.
     - Walk the class through creating an account and joining the gym’s Founding Round on the spot.
   - Pulse provides a simple, gym-branded coach script and a one-page visual guide so coaches can do this comfortably without improvising under pressure.

3. **Make “on the board” recognition contingent on Pulse check-ins.**
   - Connect Pulse usage to existing accountability mechanisms the gym already values:
     - Only workouts logged via Pulse are pulled into the in-gym leaderboard or weekly “wins” board.
     - Coaches use Pulse data when giving shout-outs in class (“10 of you logged all three sessions last week in Pulse—nice work!”) [4].
   - This ensures that logging in Pulse is the most straightforward way to be seen and celebrated, reinforcing adoption.

4. **Empower coaches with a simple pod-level dashboard for follow-up.**
   - For each affiliate gym, Pulse should surface per-class or per-coach views showing:
     - % of active members in that class who have created Pulse accounts
     - Attendance and logging streaks for the last 2–4 weeks
   - When a class’s activation percentage is below 50%, the dashboard can suggest targeted actions:
     - “Share this QR again at the start of class”
     - “Send this pre-written DM to your class group”
   - This keeps responsibility for activation with the people members trust most—the coaches—while making the actions low-effort and specific [1, 5].

5. **Run a “50% Club” challenge with visible in-gym progress.**
   - For the first 60 days, track the percentage of each gym’s active member base that has created a Pulse account and logged at least one workout.
   - Display progress both in-app and physically in the gym (e.g., a poster or whiteboard thermometer) to make the 50% goal tangible and shared [2, 4].
   - Offer a small reward or recognition when the gym hits the 50% mark (e.g., featured affiliate spotlight in Pulse’s channels, a small prize for coaches or members), reinforcing that this is a collective milestone rather than an abstract KPI.

**Why this should help reach ≥50% member activation within 60 days**

- **Coach-led, in-context onboarding** leverages the strongest predictor of member behavior change in gyms: trusted coach influence [1]. By having coaches walk members through sign-up during class, Pulse reduces dependence on low-converting channels like email.
- **Time-bound, program-integrated framing** gives a compelling “why now” tied to a concrete 6-week cycle rather than a generic app pitch [3]. Members perceive Pulse as how their gym is running the current block, not as extra homework.
- **Visible, social reinforcement** (leaderboards, boards, shout-outs) ensures that using Pulse is linked to status and recognition within the community, harnessing accountability and social identity [2, 4].
- **Simple, coach-facing activation metrics and nudges** mean gyms don’t have to invent their own growth tactics; Pulse tells coaches exactly where they stand and how to move the needle, increasing the likelihood that gyms will actually push toward the 50% activation target [5].

If even half of the gym’s regular attendees are present at a Launch Class and coached through sign-up, and follow-up classes repeat the script with stragglers, hitting ≥50% account creation by day 60 becomes a realistic, repeatable outcome rather than a one-off success.

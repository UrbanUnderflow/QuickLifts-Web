# Partnership-Led Community Growth — Recommendations by Partner Lane

This document turns the patterns from `partnership-led-community-growth-patterns.md` into concrete partnership design ideas for each lane: **Brands**, **Gyms**, and **Run Clubs**. Each recommendation ties back to one or more patterns (e.g., Community-Driven Adherence, Creator-Led Accountability, Hybrid Gym + Digital Communities, Recovery Rituals as Culture, Gap-Sensitive Engagement).

## Brands

- **Co-design 3-ritual weeks around the product (Pattern: Community-Driven Adherence).** Work with brand partners to define simple weekly templates that integrate their products into three anchor touchpoints—e.g., a training session, a recovery ritual, and a readiness check-in—so users experience the brand as part of a sustainable cadence rather than a one-off challenge or promo.

- **Creator-fronted campaigns with behavior-first goals (Pattern: Creator-Led Accountability).** Structure brand campaigns around trusted creators who commit to a specific behavior goal ("3 rituals/week for 8 weeks") and use Pulse rounds to track showing up, not aesthetic changes. The creator’s weekly check-ins and reflections become the heartbeat; Pulse provides the behavior receipts.

- **Recovery ritual series instead of extreme transformations (Pattern: Recovery Rituals as Culture).** Help brands sponsor sequences of small, repeatable recovery behaviors (sleep wind-downs, cooldown routines, easy days) that can be logged in Pulse. Frame the brand’s products as part of these rituals and measure success as reduced long gaps and more readiness check-ins, not dramatic before/after shots.

- **Gap-aware "we’ve got your back" touchpoints (Pattern: Gap-Sensitive Engagement).** When Pulse detects >3-day gaps among brand-sourced users, allow the brand’s creator or community manager to send human messages ("We all fall off; here’s a small way to start again") instead of generic push notifications. This keeps relationship-first tone while targeting behavior risks.

- **Hybrid digital + in-person activations where possible (Pattern: Hybrid Gym + Digital Communities).** For brands with retail or event presence, use Pulse rounds to extend in-person activations (pop-up runs, in-store workouts) into weekly digital rituals. Make the app the place where those experiences are remembered and built on, not just advertised.

## Gyms

- **Turn the gym’s weekly schedule into named rituals on Pulse (Pattern: Community-Driven Adherence).** Mirror the gym’s structure (e.g., "Monday strength," "Wednesday intervals," "Saturday community class") as recurring rituals in Pulse rounds, so gym members see their attendance streaks and 3+ rituals/week wins reflected in one place.

- **Let head coaches and key trainers lead digital programs (Pattern: Creator-Led Accountability).** Encourage gyms to assign specific coaches as digital program leads in Pulse, with short weekly messages, check-ins, and post-class reflections. Members then experience Pulse as an extension of their relationship with those coaches, not an external app.

- **Build recovery blocks into gym challenges (Pattern: Recovery Rituals as Culture).** When designing Pulse-backed gym challenges (e.g., 6-week strength block), include explicitly scheduled recovery elements—easy days, mobility sessions, sleep goals—and track those behaviors alongside training. Celebrate members who "trained smart" and completed recovery rituals, not just those who logged the most volume.

- **Use multi-day gap triggers to prompt coach outreach (Pattern: Gap-Sensitive Engagement).** Configure Pulse so that when a member on a gym-linked round passes a >3-day gap, the primary notification is to the gym’s coaching team (or auto-generated coach DM templates), prompting human outreach: "Haven’t seen you this week—want help adjusting the plan?"

- **Offer hybrid memberships that include a Pulse layer (Pattern: Hybrid Gym + Digital Communities).** Work with gyms to create membership tiers that bundle in Pulse access (e.g., class pack + Pulse rounds). Use the app to hold the plan between visits, track at-home sessions, and keep members connected to the community when they can’t physically attend.

## Run Clubs

- **Codify club rituals as recurring Pulse rounds (Pattern: Community-Driven Adherence).** Map the club’s rhythm—track nights, tempo runs, long runs—into Pulse rounds that repeat weekly or seasonally. Members join their sub-group’s round and see their attendance and 3-ritual weeks in the context of the club.

- **Let run leaders act as creators inside Pulse (Pattern: Creator-Led Accountability).** Give run captains the tools to post short pre-run plans, post-run debriefs, and gap-friendly encouragement in Pulse. Runners are more likely to follow programming and show up when it comes from their actual club leaders, not anonymous copy.

- **Emphasize smart loading and recovery over mileage bravado (Pattern: Recovery Rituals as Culture).** Design club-aligned Pulse programs that include cut-back weeks, easy runs, and explicit recovery rituals (sleep, nutrition, mobility) as first-class behaviors. Highlight members who listened to readiness signals and scaled appropriately, not just those who ran the most miles.

- **Use absence as a cue for community care, not shame (Pattern: Gap-Sensitive Engagement).** When run-club members miss a week or two, equip leaders with gentle, human scripts ("We missed you at track—want help finding a route or time that fits this week?") instead of automated guilt trips. Pulse can surface the gaps; leaders provide the tone and context.

- **Blend big events with ongoing micro-rounds (Patterns: Hybrid Gym + Digital Communities, Community-Driven Adherence).** Use races and flagship events as anchor moments but surround them with smaller, ongoing Pulse rounds (e.g., off-season maintenance, "winter base" programs). This ensures the club’s energy doesn’t evaporate after a single peak race but continues through year-round community touchpoints.

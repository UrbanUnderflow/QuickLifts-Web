# Partnership-Led Community Growth — Recommendations by Partner Lane

This document turns the patterns in `partnership-led-community-growth-patterns.md` into concrete partnership/community plays for Pulse’s three core lanes: **Brands**, **Gyms**, and **Run Clubs**. Each recommendation explicitly ties a pattern by name to behaviors or features Pulse can support.

## Brands

- **Creator-Led Accountability → Brand-hosted behavior journeys.** Pair each brand activation with a named creator or coach who leads an 8–12 week Pulse round focused on 3+ weekly rituals (not aesthetic outcomes). The creator posts weekly framing messages, readiness-aware scaling tips, and mid-program reflections, while Pulse tracks adherence and gap patterns.

- **Community-Driven Adherence → Co-branded rituals, not one-off stunts.** Work with brands to define 2–3 recurring rituals that already exist in their community (e.g., "Sunday reset," "midweek strength," "Friday shake-out") and mirror those as anchors in Pulse rounds. The app becomes the place where those rituals are logged and celebrated, strengthening the sense that "this is what people like us do each week."

- **Hybrid Gym + Digital Loop → Extend events into ongoing loops.** For brands that host in-person runs, pop-up classes, or product launch workouts, use Pulse to turn those events into the first step of a longer round. Participants join a brand round at the event, then continue with at-home or partner-gym sessions tracked in the app, keeping the brand present between live touchpoints.

- **Recovery Culture vs. Burnout Cycles → Recovery-forward campaigns.** Encourage brands to sponsor recovery-focused rounds (sleep hygiene, deload weeks, mobility streaks) rather than only high-intensity transformations. Pulse can surface recovery completions and reduced long gaps as key success metrics, helping brands tell stories about sustainable performance rather than extreme pushes.

- **Run-Club Ritual Stickiness → Brand x run-club collaborations.** When brands partner with run clubs, structure co-branded rounds around the club’s existing ritual spine (track night, long run) and let the brand’s creator host seasonal or race-build programs. Pulse tracks attendance and gaps, giving brands visibility into how embedded they are in the club’s weekly life.

## Gyms

- **Community-Driven Adherence → Mirror the class calendar in rounds.** For each gym, create Pulse rounds that directly map to their weekly schedule (e.g., "Mon: Strength", "Wed: Conditioning", "Sat: Community WOD"). Members join a round aligned to their membership type, and Pulse shows them how often they’re hitting their 3+ weekly touchpoints inside that community.

- **Creator-Led Accountability → Coach-led digital blocks.** Assign specific coaches as digital block owners in Pulse (e.g., "Coach Lina’s 6-week strength base"). They record short weekly check-ins, share readiness-aware modifications, and respond to gap alerts with human messages. The app holds the plan and receipts; the coach holds the relationship.

- **Hybrid Gym + Digital Loop → At-home and travel extensions.** Use Pulse to give members a way to stay in the gym’s orbit when they can’t attend in person—simple at-home sessions, mobility check-ins, or login-only "presence" rituals that maintain the habit of checking in with the gym community even during busy weeks or travel.

- **Recovery Culture vs. Burnout Cycles → Built-in deload and recovery weeks.** Design gym challenges and blocks in Pulse with explicit lower-load weeks and recovery-focused tasks (sleep goals, mobility sessions, step-based days). Surface completion of these behaviors in coach and owner dashboards so gyms see which cohorts are training smart, not just hard.

- **Run-Club Ritual Stickiness (for hybrid gyms) → Specialty run or endurance pods.** For gyms with endurance or run pods, treat those subgroups like internal run clubs: create Pulse rounds around their key sessions (tempo days, long runs) and let a lead coach/captain host. This strengthens cross-pollination between strength and endurance communities inside the same facility.

## Run Clubs

- **Run-Club Ritual Stickiness → Ritualized club rounds.** Create standing Pulse rounds that mirror the club’s core rituals (e.g., "Tuesday Track", "Thursday Tempo", "Saturday Long Run") and reset each season. Runners see their attendance patterns and gaps over time, which can be used by leaders to spot members drifting away.

- **Creator-Led Accountability → Captain-hosted seasons.** Name a captain or small leadership crew as the hosts of each Pulse season (base building, race prep, off-season). They use in-app posts to set focus for the week, normalize scaling based on readiness, and respond personally to gap alerts, turning the app into an extension of the conversations that already happen at the start line.

- **Community-Driven Adherence → Highlight belonging behavior, not just pace.** Design Pulse metrics and leaderboards for run clubs that celebrate showing up—attending key sessions, completing recovery runs, returning after gaps—rather than only fastest times or highest mileage. This helps newer and slower runners feel like full members of the community.

- **Recovery Culture vs. Burnout Cycles → Smart race-build templates.** Use Pulse to offer race-build rounds that include cut-back weeks, optional scaling, and explicit recovery tasks (sleep, nutrition check-ins, easy-run compliance). Leaders can point to these templates as club standards, reducing the social pressure to overtrain.

- **Hybrid Gym + Digital Loop → Cross-partner programs.** For run clubs that have relationships with local gyms or brands, co-design Pulse rounds that weave together club runs and gym-based strength or mobility sessions. This uses the Hybrid Gym + Digital Loop pattern to turn the app into a shared canvas across multiple community spaces.

# Partnership-Led Community Growth — Risks & Guardrails

This document captures key risks and guardrails for Pulse’s Partnership-Led Community Growth strategy, drawing on the evidence summarized in `partnership-led-community-growth-sources.md` and the patterns in `partnership-led-community-growth-patterns.md`.

- **Risk: Overclaiming health or clinical outcomes.**
  - **Example:** A brand or gym campaign is marketed as "injury-proof your training" or "get cleared to play in 6 weeks" when Pulse is only tracking sessions, gaps, and readiness check-ins ([see regulatory guidance in sources](./partnership-led-community-growth-sources.md)).
  - **Guardrail:** Anchor all partner-facing copy in behavioral and readiness outcomes—showing up, shortening gaps, building rituals, noticing readiness—rather than diagnoses, cures, or guarantees. Route high-stakes language through a review step against internal red/green language rails and external digital health guidance before launch.

- **Risk: Creator misalignment and unsustainable programming.**
  - **Example:** A creator or coach, incentivized to drive short-term engagement, launches a "no days off" or extreme deficit challenge that boosts early participation but conflicts with the Recovery Culture vs. Burnout Cycles pattern and leads to fatigue and dropout.
  - **Guardrail:** Bake behavioral and recovery-oriented KPIs into creator briefs and Pulse dashboards—3+ rituals/week, reduced 7–10+ day gaps, completion of recovery behaviors. Require that partner rounds include explicit recovery elements and give Pulse veto power on formats that contradict the evidence base in `partnership-led-community-growth-sources.md`.

- **Risk: Community and challenge fatigue.**
  - **Example:** A gym, brand, and run club all stack overlapping, high-intensity rounds over several months, creating a constant sense of pressure and eroding enjoyment, especially for members with work or caregiving responsibilities.
  - **Guardrail:** Use a seasonal planning view inside Pulse to limit how many high-load rounds can run concurrently for a given cohort. Encourage partners to alternate between heavier pushes and lighter, recovery- or skill-focused micro-rounds, drawing on the Recovery Culture vs. Burnout Cycles pattern and practitioner insights about challenge fatigue.

- **Risk: Treating gaps as failure instead of context.**
  - **Example:** A runner misses two weeks due to illness and receives a generic "you’re falling behind" notification from a co-branded round, making them feel judged and less likely to re-engage.
  - **Guardrail:** Design gap detection as a **relationship signal**, not a scolding mechanism. Use Pulse to surface >3-day and >7-day gaps to human leaders (creators, coaches, captains) with suggested scripts that normalize pauses and offer small restart steps, consistent with the Gap-Sensitive Engagement and Run-Club Ritual Stickiness patterns.

- **Risk: Flattening diverse partner cultures into generic templates.**
  - **Example:** A CrossFit box, a boutique spin studio, and a trail-focused run club all receive the same 30-day "shred" template, eroding what makes each community feel distinct and limiting buy-in.
  - **Guardrail:** Treat the shared patterns—Creator-Led Accountability, Community-Driven Adherence, Hybrid Gym + Digital Loop, Recovery Culture vs. Burnout Cycles—as a *spine*, then deliberately localize naming, imagery, and rituals for each partner. Require at least one custom ritual or narrative element per partner lane in any co-designed Pulse round.

- **Risk: Data privacy and trust erosion.**
  - **Example:** A partner requests granular, personally identifiable behavior data (e.g., individual readiness scores or gap histories) for marketing, risking user trust and conflicting with privacy expectations.
  - **Guardrail:** Limit partner-facing analytics to aggregated, de-identified behavior metrics (e.g., percentage of members hitting 3+ rituals/week, distribution of gap lengths) and be explicit in partner contracts and UI copy about what is and is not shared. Align Pulse’s data flows with its privacy docs and `partnership-led-community-growth-sources.md` discussions of regulatory boundaries.

- **Risk: Over-reliance on the app without human anchors.**
  - **Example:** A brand or gym launches a Pulse integration with rich features but no named host or community leader; users download the app, explore briefly, and then disengage because no one is visibly "holding" the experience.
  - **Guardrail:** Make human fronting—Creator-Led Accountability—a requirement for key partner activations. Every marquee round should have a clearly identified host (coach, creator, captain) whose messages and presence are woven into the experience, with Pulse acting as the infrastructure rather than the star.

- **Risk: Neglecting less visible or lower-capacity members.**
  - **Example:** Dashboards and stories focus only on top performers or high-volume members, while those building from low activity levels or returning after long gaps feel unseen and drift away.
  - **Guardrail:** Design partner dashboards and storytelling prompts that highlight a mix of members: consistent attenders, smart scalers, and successful returners after gaps. Use Pulse’s behavior data to surface these diverse wins and encourage partners to feature them, reinforcing Community-Driven Adherence as something available at all intensity levels.

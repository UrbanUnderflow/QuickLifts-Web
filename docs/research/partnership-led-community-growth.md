# Market Overview

Below is an initial list of platforms and ecosystems that lean heavily on partnerships and communities. Later sections will go deeper on their models and tactics.

- **Strava** — Social fitness network built around clubs, segments, and challenges; partners with brands, events, and clubs to run sponsored challenges and grow communities.
  - URL: https://www.strava.com

- **Nike Run Club (NRC) / Nike Training Club (NTC)** — Brand-led training and run-club ecosystem where coaches, local run clubs, and retail partners create community experiences tied to Nike products and programs.
  - URL: https://www.nike.com/nrc-app

- **Adidas Runners** — Global run-club network with city captains, weekly runs, and race build-ups, often linking brand campaigns to local community crews.
  - URL: https://www.adidas.com/adidasrunners

- **Peloton** — Connected fitness platform where star instructors, member communities, and occasional brand/artist collaborations drive recurring programs and challenges.
  - URL: https://www.onepeloton.com

- **ClassPass** — Aggregator of partner gyms and studios, using its partner network and member subscriptions to drive class discovery, attendance, and habit formation.
  - URL: https://classpass.com

- **Mindbody** — SaaS platform and consumer marketplace for gyms and studios; growth is driven by thousands of partner businesses and integrations with other wellness tools.
  - URL: https://www.mindbodyonline.com

- **Whoop** — Wearable + subscription platform that leans on creator partnerships, teams, and community challenges to turn recovery and strain metrics into shared experiments.
  - URL: https://www.whoop.com

- **Oura** — Readiness and sleep ring used heavily in wellness and performance communities, amplified by creators running habit and lifestyle challenges.
  - URL: https://ouraring.com

- **Zwift** — Virtual cycling and running platform built on group rides, races, clubs, and brand/event partnerships.
  - URL: https://www.zwift.com

- **Garmin + Garmin Connect** — Wearable ecosystem whose clubs, challenges, and integrations with event organizers, run clubs, and training platforms make it a key partner hub.
  - URL: https://connect.garmin.com

## Key Competitors

Below are brief profiles of selected platforms from the Market Overview that are most relevant to Pulse’s partnership-led community strategy. All specific metrics are **Unverified** until backed by primary sources.

### Strava

- **What it is:** A social fitness network where athletes track activities and engage via segments, clubs, and challenges.
- **Partnership focus:**
  - Brand-sponsored challenges (e.g., distance/elevation challenges, streak campaigns) that offer badges and sometimes physical/discount rewards.
  - Tools for run clubs and event organizers to create Strava clubs and official event challenges.
- **Community connection:**
  - Clubs and challenges create lightweight structures for recurring participation; partners provide the narrative and distribution, Strava provides the infrastructure.
  - Many run clubs and race organizers treat Strava as their de facto digital hub [Unverified].

### Nike Run Club (NRC) / Nike Training Club (NTC)

- **What it is:** Nike’s primary digital training ecosystem, combining coaching content, tracking, and run-club integrations.
- **Partnership focus:**
  - Local NRC chapters co-run with stores, coaches, and sometimes partner gyms.
  - Co-branded events and race campaigns where Nike provides programs and community narratives, while local partners host IRL runs.
- **Community connection:**
  - Combines digital training plans with local run communities; the app acts as a shared reference for workouts and achievements.

### Peloton

- **What it is:** A connected fitness platform anchored around high-production classes and instructors-as-creators.
- **Partnership focus:**
  - Internal creator partnerships: instructors with their own followings lead named programs and series.
  - Brand and artist collaborations (e.g., music series, theme rides) that function as co-branded events.
- **Community connection:**
  - Leaderboards and live class chat create synchronized community experiences.
  - Instructor fandom and recurring programs drive medium- and long-term engagement [Unverified].

### ClassPass

- **What it is:** A subscription marketplace that aggregates thousands of partner gyms and studios.
- **Partnership focus:**
  - B2B partnerships with studios/gyms, historically via credit-based attendance and revenue share.
  - Core pitch: incremental demand, marketing exposure, and utilization of off-peak capacity.
- **Community connection:**
  - Community is primarily local (members feel belonging at their studios); ClassPass amplifies access and habit formation through variety and ease of discovery.

### Mindbody

- **What it is:** Business management and booking software for gyms and studios, plus a consumer-facing marketplace.
- **Partnership focus:**
  - Deep B2B integrations (scheduling, payments, CRM) with partner studios.
  - Integration partnerships with other tools (marketing, payments, wellness apps) [Unverified].
- **Community connection:**
  - Mindbody is largely infrastructure; community energy lives at the studio level.

## Partnership Models

This section summarizes, at a high level, how selected platforms structure and operationalize partnerships. All details are **Unverified** until validated with primary sources.

### Strava – Sponsored Challenges & Club Infrastructure

- **Brand partnerships:**
  - Brands sponsor challenges that any Strava user can join; incentives include digital badges and sometimes physical rewards or discount codes.
  - Revenue is likely driven by a mix of subscription, brand activations, and data products [Unverified].
- **Run club & event partnerships:**
  - Clubs can be created by anyone; official partnerships with races and run clubs allow them to host event-specific challenges and aggregate training.
- **Community effect:**
  - Sponsored challenges give brands immediate access to an enormous existing user base, while clubs/segments keep engagement localized and social.

### Nike Run Club (NRC) – Brand + Local Community Hybrid

- **Brand partnerships:**
  - NRC/NTC serve as the digital layer for Nike’s brand campaigns (e.g., shoe launches, race sponsorships).
  - Collaborations with race organizers and city partners turn NRC programs into event build-ups.
- **Gym/club partnerships:**
  - Local NRC groups often rely on coaches or partner gyms to host weekly runs and community events.
- **Community effect:**
  - Members see NRC as the connective tissue between the Nike brand, training content, and their local run club.

### Peloton – Internal Creator Ecosystem and Brand Collabs

- **Creator partnerships:**
  - Instructors are effectively in-house creators with strong personal brands.
  - Program and series structures give these creators recurring slots and cohesive narratives.
- **Brand/artist partnerships:**
  - Co-branded classes (music artists, cultural moments) act as high-attention events.
- **Community effect:**
  - Users cluster around favorite instructors and series; partnerships provide fresh hooks for lapsed users to return.

### ClassPass – Studio/Gym Partner Network

- **Studio/gym partnerships:**
  - Studios join the ClassPass network to get additional demand and marketing exposure.
  - Commercial terms historically involved variable per-visit payouts tied to ClassPass credits [Unverified].
- **Community effect:**
  - ClassPass doesn’t own community in a deep sense; instead, it turns partner gyms into accessible on-ramps, making it easier for users to experiment and find a "home" community.

### Mindbody – B2B Infra + Marketplace

- **Studio/gym partnerships:**
  - Gyms/studios are the primary customers; they pay for business management and booking tools.
  - Mindbody’s app surfaces these partners to consumers as a directory/marketplace.
- **Integration partnerships:**
  - Connects with third-party tools (marketing platforms, payments, etc.), forming a broader wellness ecosystem.
- **Community effect:**
  - The community is owned by the gyms/studios; Mindbody enables them rather than hosting community itself.

# Pulse Health Narrative Spine — Audience & Purpose

## Who This Spine Is For

This document is an internal guardrail for how Pulse talks about health and performance across all partner-facing surfaces. It is written primarily for:

- **Founders & Product Leadership** – to keep the product story and health promise consistent as we ship features, negotiate partnerships, and make roadmap tradeoffs.
- **Business Development & Partnerships (BD)** – to ground every partner conversation in a clear, behavior-only promise and shared metric language, so we don’t oversell outcomes or drift into clinical territory.
- **Brand, Marketing, and Content** – to give writers, designers, and creators a shared spine of phrases, metaphors, and constraints they can plug into campaigns, landing pages, and in-app copy without re-litigating safety lines every time.
- **Legal, Compliance, and Medical Advisors** – to quickly see the outer edges of what Pulse will and won’t say, so they can review campaigns and partnership concepts against a stable, pre-aligned narrative frame instead of one-off copy lines.
- **Community & Customer Experience** – to ensure coaches, moderators, and support teams describe Pulse’s role in people’s health in ways that are accurate, de-risked, and emotionally honest when they’re answering questions in chats, DMs, and events.

## What This Spine Is For

The Health Narrative Spine does four things:

1. **Locks a behavior-only health promise.** It articulates, in plain language, that Pulse exists to help people show up for key behaviors (training, recovery, readiness rituals) and close motivation gaps — not to diagnose, treat, cure, or clear anyone to play.
2. **Defines three core behavior metrics.** It names and explains the three behaviors Pulse cares about most (multi-day gaps, weekly key rituals, and readiness behaviors) in human terms that can travel across brands, gyms, and run clubs.
3. **Sets a red/green language rail.** It lists phrases we actively lean on (green) and phrases we never use (red) so that any partner-safe copy can be sanity-checked quickly without needing a specialist every time.
4. **Provides ready-made narrative lines by ICP.** It offers plug-and-play example lines tailored to three core ICPs (brands, gyms, run clubs) so Community Playbooks and partner teams can move fast while staying inside our safety lines.

## Where This Spine Should Be Used

This spine is a **source document**, not a public artifact. It should be referenced whenever we are:

- **Building or updating decks**
  - Company overview and fundraising decks
  - BD and partnership pitch decks
  - Co-branded campaign or challenge proposals
- **Writing Community Playbooks and partner materials**
  - The three ICU Community Playbooks (brands, gyms, run clubs)
  - Creator briefs and partner one-pagers
  - Challenge descriptions and onboarding flows
- **Drafting outreach and ongoing communication**
  - Cold and warm BD outreach emails
  - Follow-up notes and recap emails after partner calls
  - In-app announcements and partner launch messages
- **Reviewing campaigns for safety and alignment**
  - Legal/compliance review of marketing claims
  - QA checks on in-app copy, landing pages, and paid media
  - Creator script reviews for sponsored content

In practice, this means:

- **Founders and BD** pull language directly from the behavior promise and example lines when they describe Pulse in meetings, decks, and emails.
- **Brand and Content** use the red/green list as a hard constraint for any health- or outcome-adjacent copy, especially when translating between different ICPs.
- **Legal and medical advisors** review the spine first, then spot-check artifacts (decks, campaigns, scripts) for deviations instead of re-arguing fundamentals.

Future sections of this document will add:
- The finalized behavior-only promise
- Plain-language definitions of the three core metrics
- The red/green language list
- ICP-specific example lines for brands, gyms, and run clubs

Those sections will live in this same file so every team is working from a single, shared narrative spine.

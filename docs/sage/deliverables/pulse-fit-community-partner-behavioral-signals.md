# Pulse-fit Community Partner Behavioral Signals — Shortlisted Candidates (Step 3/5)

**Scope.** This document takes 5–7 shortlisted examples per ICP (Brands, Gyms/Studios, Run Clubs) from the broad candidate pool and captures **behavioral signals** relevant to Pulse:

- Creator ecosystem structure (who is actually creating, at what rough scale)
- Challenge / activation cadence (how often they run things over time, not just stunts)
- Join vs finish patterns (where visible, or hypotheses + evidence TODOs)
- Primary community channels (IG, app, WhatsApp, Strava, Discord, etc.)

> **Evidence status note:** Because live web tooling is currently unavailable in this environment, all behavioral descriptions below are based on **general public information and ecosystem patterns up to ~2024**. Treat them as **Unverified** until we attach specific URLs, screenshots, or campaign pages in a later pass.

---

## 1. Brands — Shortlist Behavioral Signals

Shortlist focus: **Nike Run Club, Adidas Runners, WHOOP, Oura, Strava, Peloton**.

### 1.1 Nike Run Club (NRC)

**Creator Ecosystem (Unverified)**
- **Types:** Nike coaches, sponsored athletes, and local NRC run leaders; plus a long tail of runners posting NRC run summaries and screenshots.
- **Scale (rough):** Thousands of independent creators globally who occasionally tag NRC; a smaller core of Nike-backed coaches/creators who appear repeatedly.
- **Pattern:** Content tends to cluster around training blocks (e.g., marathon seasons), new app features, and city-specific events.

**Challenge / Activation Cadence (Unverified)**
- Runs **recurring app-based challenges and training plans**, often tied to:
  - Major global races (e.g., marquee marathons)
  - Seasonal themes (e.g., “Start Running” plans, 5K/10K build-ups)
- Playbook pattern: on-ramp plans, intermediate plans, and race-specific plans surfaced in the NRC app.

**Join vs Finish Patterns (Hypothesis)**
- Likely exhibits typical training-plan drop-off curves: high sign-up, lower plan completion.
- Public signals to check in a future pass:
  - Completion badge showcases or finishers’ posts around the end of official training cycles.
  - App reviews/Reddit threads discussing finishing NRC programs vs dropping early.

**Primary Community Channels (Unverified)**
- NRC mobile **app** (core training + tracking hub).
- **Instagram**: @nikerunning / @nikeplus plus city-level handles.
- **Local NRC run clubs** coordinated via email, local IG pages, and potentially WhatsApp/FB groups (chapter-specific).

### 1.2 Adidas Runners

**Join vs Finish Patterns (Hypothesis)**

- Signals to validate later:
  - Captains’ recap posts about finishers or end-of-season celebrations.
  - Strava club stats or public participation numbers (if accessible).

**Primary Community Channels (Unverified)**

## 4. How to Use This in Steps 4–5

- **Step 4 (Fit Tiering):** Use these behavioral notes as a starting point to determine **Flagship-ready vs Future-fit**, but do **not** treat any behavioral claim as confirmed until at least one primary source (URL, screenshot, or campaign page) is attached.
- **Step 5 (A-list Docs):** When building ICP-specific A-list pages, reference this file for:
  - Which behavioral levers each partner naturally already uses (streaks, leaderboards, race cycles).
  - Where Pulse can plug into existing behavior instead of inventing new rituals.

In a future pass, each partner that graduates onto the A-list should have:

1. At least **3 concrete, source-cited behavioral examples** (posts, challenge pages, app screenshots).
2. A clear statement of **evidence status** (Verified vs Unverified) for key claims.
3. A short mapping from existing behaviors → Pulse challenge mechanics.

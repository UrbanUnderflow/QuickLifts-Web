# Pulse-fit Community Partner Candidate Pool — Brands, Gyms/Studios, Run Clubs (Broad Pass 1)

**Scope (Step 2/5).** This document assembles a broad candidate pool for three ICPs — **Brands**, **Gyms/Studios**, and **Run Clubs** — and quickly screens each against the Pulse-fit criteria defined in `pulse-fit-community-partner-criteria.md`. This is a *first-pass map*, not a final A-list.

- Target breadth: ~15–25 candidates per ICP.
- Goal: Remove obviously misaligned / purely aesthetic picks, surface a workable shortlist for deeper behavioral research in Steps 3–5.
- Important: None of the entities below should be treated as Pulse leads or prospects unless/until they appear in `docs/partnership/lead-source-of-truth.md`. Where relevant, I explicitly mark Pulse-related claims as `Unverified`.

> Note: This pass is built from general market knowledge (fitness/wellness ecosystem patterns) and internal prospect notes where available. It is **not** asserting partnership interest.

---

## 1. Brands — Broad Candidate Pool (First-pass Screen)

### 1.1 Screened-in (Behaviorally Pulse-fit on First Pass)

These brands exhibit clear creator ecosystems, recurring challenge/activation behavior, and identifiable community hubs. They are **behavioral matches**, not confirmed Pulse leads.

1. **Nike Run Club (NRC)**  — *Global run-focused ecosystem*
   - **Why in the pool:** Longstanding app-based run community with recurring challenges, creator/coach content, and global run clubs. Strong fit to run + habit-building narrative.
   - **Signals:** Structured training plans, seasonal challenges, and city-based communities; app + IG + local clubs.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong behavioral fit for Run Club/Brand hybrid experiments.

2. **Adidas Runners**  — *Brand-backed global run community*
   - **Why in the pool:** Community-centric running ecosystem with captains, chapters, and training cycles.
   - **Signals:** Training seasons tied to major races; IG/Strava presence; regular group runs and challenges.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for run-based challenges + co-created seasons.

3. **Whoop**  — *Wearable recovery and performance platform*
   - **Why in the pool:** Always-on performance + recovery tracking, heavy creator and athlete ecosystem, and in-app/team challenges.
   - **Signals:** Creator-led breakdowns of recovery/strain; team-based leaderboards; seasonal challenges.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for data-integrated challenges and creator-led performance experiments.

4. **Oura**  — *Sleep and readiness wearable*
   - **Why in the pool:** Large existing creator ecosystem sharing readiness/sleep data; community challenges around sleep, recovery, and routines.
   - **Signals:** IG/YouTube creators regularly posting Oura screenshots; brand run seasonal campaigns.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for recovery-focused, habit-based challenges.

5. **Garmin (Garmin Connect + Garmin Coach)**  — *Endurance + wearable ecosystem*
   - **Why in the pool:** Deep running/cycling/triathlon community integrated via Garmin Connect, training plans, and challenges.
   - **Signals:** Strava-integrated community, badges, and structured programs; huge endurance creator base.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for endurance-focused behavior loops.

6. **Strava**  — *Social fitness network*
   - **Why in the pool:** Native social graph, segments, clubs, and challenges across multiple sports.
   - **Signals:** Clubs, monthly challenges, leaderboards, creator clubs; robust habit tracking.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for community + challenge primitives; overlaps heavily with Pulse’s behavior model.

7. **Peloton**  — *Connected fitness brand with strong instructor creators*
   - **Why in the pool:** Highly engaged instructor-creator ecosystem; class series, programs, and badges.
   - **Signals:** Programs with completion tracking, instructor-led challenges, large social media footprints.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** High behavioral alignment; integration complexity is high but conceptually strong.

8. **WHOOP-affiliated creator collectives (e.g., endurance/functional fitness influencers)**  — *Creator-first layer on top of wearables*
   - **Why in the pool:** Individual creators already running informal challenges using wearable data.
   - **Signals:** Screenshot-based posts, recovery challenges, sleep or load competitions.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong at the creator level; brand-level coordination would need mapping.

9. **Calm / Headspace (movement-adjacent mental wellness)**  — *Habit-based wellness platforms*
   - **Why in the pool:** Structured streaks, programs, and community challenges around habits (meditation, sleep).
   - **Signals:** Streak tracking, themed programs, periodic community challenges.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Behaviorally relevant where Pulse narrative includes mental training/consistency.

10. **Tonals / Tempo / Forme-type strength hardware brands**  — *Connected strength training systems*
    - **Why in the pool:** Structured strength programs, form tracking, instructor ecosystems.
    - **Signals:** Program-based challenges, completion tracking, instructor or ambassador promotion.
    - **Quick fit verdict:** Good behavioral fit where creator ecosystems exist; needs brand-by-brand validation.

### 1.2 Screened-out or Edge Cases (for now)

These brands are either too aesthetics-heavy, lack visible community hubs, or operate in regulatory/brand-alignment gray zones.

- **Detox/rapid-weight-loss brands (generic category)**
  - **Reason:** Heavy aesthetic/fad framing, questionable claims, low alignment with Pulse’s safety narrative.

- **Supplement brands with aggressive fat-loss/testosterone claims (generic category)**
  - **Reason:** Regulatory gray areas and brand-safety mismatch.

- **Purely content-platform fitness apps with no visible community or challenge structures**
  - **Reason:** Behavior looks like static libraries, not community or challenge ecosystems.

> These categories stay **out of scope** unless a specific brand shows clear, verifiable deviation from the general pattern.

## 2. Gyms / Studios — Broad Candidate Pool (First-pass Screen)

### 2.1 Screened-in (Behaviorally Pulse-fit on First Pass)

1. **Barry’s Bootcamp**  — *High-intensity class network with strong brand culture*
   - **Why in the pool:** Global studio network, challenge culture (e.g., seasonal series), and social content from instructors and members.
   - **Signals:** Class packs, seasonal challenges, IG-based community content, local studio events.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for high-energy, challenge-based campaigns.

2. **Orangetheory Fitness**  — *Heart-rate-based group training franchise*
   - **Why in the pool:** Built-in metrics, heart-rate zones, and benchmark days; runs recurring challenges.
   - **Signals:** “Dri-Tri”, benchmark events, studio-level challenges, app-based tracking.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for behavior tracking and data-integrated challenges.

3. **F45 Training**  — *Functional training franchise with challenge culture*
   - **Why in the pool:** Known for 8-week challenges, global network, and heavy social share behavior.
   - **Signals:** Global challenge campaigns, nutrition + training blocks, member transformations.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for habit-based challenges and cohort experiences.

4. **CrossFit-affiliated boxes (select)**  — *Community-first strength/conditioning gyms*
   - **Why in the pool:** Strong local communities; The Open, in-house challenges, and coach creator content.
   - **Signals:** Annual Open plus in-house throwdowns; WOD posts, member spotlights, IG/FB groups.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for micro-community pilots; needs selection of specific, inclusive boxes.

5. **Rumble / Shadowbox-type boxing studios**  — *Boutique boxing/conditioning*
   - **Why in the pool:** Class-based, personality-driven instructors, social content.
   - **Signals:** Themed classes, seasonal campaigns, instructor content.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Medium-strong; good for “rounds” narratives and challenge arcs.

6. **Solidcore / reformer-style studios**  — *Low-impact, high-intensity group classes*
   - **Why in the pool:** Programmed progressions, membership model, and highly engaged communities.
   - **Signals:** Multi-week program arcs, waitlists, IG content featuring member journeys.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Good fit for habit-building and streak narratives.

7. **Local hybrid performance gyms with content-forward coaches (generic archetype)**
   - **Why in the pool:** Many cities have 1–2 standout performance gyms where owners/coaches act as creators.
   - **Signals:** Regular technique content, program announcements, WhatsApp/FB groups for members.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong at local pilot level; requires city-specific scouting.

### 2.2 Screened-out / deprioritized (first pass)

- **Purely aesthetic transformation studios**
  - **Reason:** Heavy emphasis on before/after images, crash diets, and short-term cuts.

- **High-end exclusive clubs with little digital footprint**
  - **Reason:** Hard to validate community behavior; more status-driven than community-driven.

- **Studios with visible culture of shaming or extreme diet practices**
  - **Reason:** Misaligned with Pulse’s wellness + community posture.

## 3. Run Clubs — Broad Candidate Pool (First-pass Screen)

### 3.1 Screened-in (Behaviorally Pulse-fit on First Pass)

1. **Nike Run Club chapters (city-level)**  — *Brand-affiliated community runs*
   - **Why in the pool:** Localized groups with regular runs, training cycles, and coach/creator presence.
   - **Signals:** Weekly runs, training blocks towards common races, local IG pages and chats.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong as reference archetype; brand overlay already in ecosystem.

2. **Adidas Runners city clubs**  — *Chapter-based global run community*
   - **Why in the pool:** Structured training seasons and captains; rich social content.
   - **Signals:** Pace groups, race-day build-ups, WhatsApp/Telegram groups in some cities.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for structured, multi-city experiments.

3. **November Project**  — *Free fitness movement with strong community identity*
   - **Why in the pool:** Consistent early-morning workouts, city-based leaders, strong accountability culture.
   - **Signals:** Hashtags, roll calls, weather-proof meetups, FB/IG groups and chats.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for attendance and streak-based experiments.

4. **Bridge Runners / Black Roses NYC / Run Dem Crew-type urban clubs**  — *Culture-forward city crews*
   - **Why in the pool:** Strong cultural identity, social media presence, and training cycles around races.
   - **Signals:** IG storytelling, race prep cycles, post-run hangs, often WhatsApp/Signal groups.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for culture + community narratives; needs club-by-club screening.

5. **Tracksmith community runs / Amateur running collectives**  — *Brand-adjacent amateur racing culture*
   - **Why in the pool:** Organized training groups, pop-up events, and creators in the amateur running space.
   - **Signals:** Seasonal training plans, race campaigns, Strava groups.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong for serious recreational runners; more performance-leaning.

6. **Local city run clubs with broadcast + group chat presence (generic archetype)**
   - **Why in the pool:** Many mid-sized clubs run weekly runs, have IG pages, and coordinate via WhatsApp/Telegram.
   - **Signals:** Weekly run posts, route announcements, group chat links, race season cycles.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Ideal for early Pulse pilots in specific cities.

7. **Charity and cause-based run clubs**  — *Community anchored by cause*
   - **Why in the pool:** Strong motivation and community bonds; regular events and training cycles.
   - **Signals:** Fundraising campaigns, team training plans, race-day coordination.
   - **Quick fit verdict:** Strong community cohesion; need cause alignment review case-by-case.

### 3.2 Screened-out / deprioritized (first pass)

- **Single-influencer “run meetups” without stable identity**
  - **Reason:** Fragile communities; behavior depends entirely on one person’s schedule.

- **Event-only pop-up runs without ongoing cadence**
  - **Reason:** No durable habit loop, harder to tie into Pulse’s ongoing engagement story.

# Pulse-fit Community Partner Criteria — Brands, Gyms, Run Clubs

**Purpose.** Define explicit, behavior-based criteria for what makes a partner "Pulse-fit" across three ICPs — **Brands**, **Gyms/Studios**, and **Run Clubs** — so that future research and outreach use the same lens. These criteria will govern which candidates make it onto the Pulse-fit Community Partners A-list and how we tag them as Flagship-ready vs Future-fit.

---

## Shared Pulse-fit Principles (All ICPs)

Regardless of category, a Pulse-fit partner should:

1. **Have a real creator ecosystem, not just a marketing team**
   - Evidence of recurring content from *independent* creators (not only the brand account) in the last 90 days.
   - Creators appear to have genuine audiences (comment quality, saves/shares, not just likes on giveaway posts).

2. **Show challenge / activation behavior**
   - Runs challenges, cohorts, streaks, events, or campaigns where participants are asked to do something over time.
   - At least some evidence of structured participation (e.g., hashtags, sign-up pages, in-app modules, or offline accountability).

3. **Have a clearly located community hub**
   - We can point to where the community *actually* lives today (e.g., IG/FB group, Discord/Slack, WhatsApp/Signal, in-app community, IRL + group chat).
   - The hub shows recent activity (posts/comments/messages within the last 30–60 days).

4. **Align with Pulse’s movement, wellness, and community mission**
   - No obvious conflict with Pulse’s health standards (e.g., aggressive fat-loss claims, gray-area supplements, unsafe training practices).
   - Messaging is effort- and habit-oriented, not purely aesthetic or body-shaming.

5. **Show signals of operational consistency**
   - Repeated activations (not just a single stunt) and a stable identity over the last 6–12 months.
   - Publicly visible systems or patterns (e.g., seasonal challenges, recurring programs, weekly runs) that could plug into Pulse.

**Global Disqualifiers (any ICP)**

- Community touchpoints are mostly dead (no visible activity in last 60–90 days).
- Only evidence of “community” is paid ads or one-off giveaway campaigns.
- Heavy reliance on unverified or non-compliant claims (e.g., miracle transformations, medical claims without backing).
- No discernible creator ecosystem beyond the brand’s own handle.
- Culture tone that clashes with Pulse (e.g., shaming, bro-science extremism, toxic competitiveness).

## ICP 1: Brands (Consumer Fitness, Wellness, Wearables, Recovery)

### Pulse-fit Criteria — Brands

**1. Creator Ecosystem**
- At least **5–10 recurring creators** (ambassadors, affiliates, or UGC creators) posting about the brand over the last 90 days.
- Mix of content types: training clips, routines, challenges, day-in-the-life, not just static product shots.
- At least some creators whose audiences are clearly **fitness- or performance-oriented**, not only lifestyle aesthetics.

**2. Challenge / Activation Behavior**
- Evidence of **challenge-style campaigns** in the last 12 months:
  - Hashtag-based challenges (e.g., 30-day habits, seasonal training blocks).
  - App-based programs with structured progression.
  - Multi-week ambassador-led campaigns.
- At least one campaign where completion / follow-through is emphasized (e.g., finishers shout-outs, progress check-ins, milestone badges).

**3. Community Location Clarity**
- We can identify **where the brand’s community gathers and talks**:
  - IG/FB groups, Discords, or Slack communities.
  - In-app social/community features.
  - Offline communities (events) that clearly route to a digital home (WhatsApp, FB groups, etc.).
- Community hub shows:
  - Posts or conversations within last 30–45 days.
  - Evidence of member-to-member interaction (not just brand broadcasting).

**4. Brand & Safety Alignment**
- Products and messaging fit comfortably within Pulse’s **movement + wellness + community** frame:
  - Training, recovery, wearables, and evidence-aligned supplements.
  - Messaging focused on consistency, performance, and habit-building.
- No obvious regulatory red flags (e.g., banned compounds, overtly deceptive health claims) on a quick scan.

**5. Integration Potential**
- Clear opportunities to **co-create challenges** that map to Pulse primitives:
  - Streak-based adherence, cohort challenges, creator-led programs.
  - Data/metric hooks (steps, HRV, runs, workouts, recovery scores) if a tech/wearable brand.
- Brand already collaborates with creators in ways that resemble **mini Pulse use cases** (e.g., creators running programs/challenges for the brand’s audience).

### Brand Disqualifiers

- Influencer posts are almost entirely **one-off sponsorships** with no recurring narrative or longitudinal engagement.
- Community is limited to **comments on brand posts**; no hub where members interact with each other.
- Challenges exist only as **purely aesthetic or gimmicky stunts** (e.g., 7-day shred, detox teas, extreme fad diets).
- Brand identity is too fragmented or rebranded repeatedly in the last 12–18 months, making it hard to anchor a durable partnership.

## ICP 2: Gyms / Studios

(Commercial gyms, boutique studios, hybrid online/IRL training spaces.)

### Pulse-fit Criteria — Gyms/Studios

**1. Creator & Coach Activity**
- At least **3–5 coaches or members** regularly creating content tied to the gym/studio:
  - Technique breakdowns, class clips, progression updates, or member spotlights.
  - Evidence that at least some creators have audiences beyond the gym’s own followers.
- Gym leverages creators/coaches to **promote programs or challenges**, not just general brand awareness.

**2. Challenge / Programming Behavior**
- Active use of:
  - 4–12 week programs, seasonal blocks, or ongoing progression cycles.
  - Member or cohort challenges (e.g., attendance streaks, lift PR blocks, run prep cycles).
  - Leaderboards, check-ins, or milestone recognition (digital or on-premise).
- Evidence that **members actually participate**, not just sign up:
  - Before/after attendance pics, weekly recaps, completion celebrations, or shout-outs.

**3. Community Location Clarity**
- We can see where **members coordinate and stay accountable**:
  - WhatsApp/Signal groups, IG Close Friends lists, FB groups, Discord, in-app community.
  - Offline events (runs, meetups, throwdowns) that funnel into a persistent communication channel.
- Group has visible recent activity (messages, announcements, or member posts within last 30 days).

**4. Cultural Alignment**
- Training culture is **supportive, inclusive, and progress-focused**:
  - Emphasis on showing up, getting better, and celebrating wins.
  - No obvious culture of shaming, crash dieting, or unsafe practices.
- Programming style (strength, conditioning, hybrid, endurance) is compatible with **Pulse’s movement-first product story**.

**5. Operational Readiness**
- Gym/studio shows the ability to **run structured campaigns** start-to-finish:
  - Clear sign-up flows, communication rhythm, and completion recognition.
- There’s an obvious point-person (owner, head coach, community manager) who orchestrates community experiments.

### Gym/Studio Disqualifiers

- The only “community” evidence is **solo social posts** by the owner; no member-to-member connection.
- Challenges are purely **sales hooks** (e.g., 6-week challenge with before/after photos) with no ongoing habit support.
- No visible group communications or all channels appear dormant.
- Coaching style appears misaligned with Pulse (e.g., publicly shaming clients, extreme diet culture).

## ICP 3: Run Clubs

(Including community run clubs, brand-affiliated run groups, and hybrid IRL/digital run communities.)

### Pulse-fit Criteria — Run Clubs

**1. Creator & Leader Ecosystem**
- At least **2–3 visible leaders or captains** regularly creating content around the club’s runs, training cycles, or member stories.
- Some members independently post about the club using recurring hashtags or location tags.
- Content shows **structured training arcs** (e.g., race build-ups, seasons), not just casual selfies.

**2. Challenge / Seasonality Behavior**
- Clear evidence of **seasons, cycles, or event-based blocks**:
  - 5K/10K/half/full training cycles.
  - Monthly streaks, mileage challenges, or theme runs.
- At least occasional use of **accountability mechanics**:
  - Check-in posts, attendance tracking, mileage boards, or shared training plans.

**3. Community Location Clarity**
- We can identify **the primary coordination channel**:
  - WhatsApp/Signal/Telegram groups.
  - IG broadcast channels, FB groups, Strava clubs, or Discord.
- Channel shows recent activity:
  - Announcements (weekly run details, route info).
  - Member replies, RSVPs, or post-run recaps.

**4. Culture & Accessibility**
- Club culture emphasizes **inclusivity and progression** over raw pace:
  - Mention of pace groups, no-drop policies, or beginner-friendliness is a plus.
- Club frames running as a **habit and social anchor**, which is aligned with Pulse’s community-forward story.

**5. Experimentation Potential**
- Club has either:
  - Already run digital or hybrid challenges (e.g., virtual races, multi-city mileage challenges), **or**
  - Shows an appetite for structured experiments (trying new formats, collaborating with brands/creators).

### Run Club Disqualifiers

- Club is essentially a **single-influencer meetup** with no broader community identity.
- No visible digital hub; coordination appears ad-hoc via stories only.
- Culture is obviously **exclusive or elitist** (e.g., only welcomes specific pace ranges, hostile to beginners) in a way that conflicts with Pulse.
- All activity appears one-off around a single event, with no sign of consistent, ongoing runs.

## Fit Tiers: Flagship-ready vs Future-fit

These tiers will be applied on top of the criteria above when evaluating specific candidates.

### Flagship-ready

A partner is **Flagship-ready** when:

- They **clearly meet** the Pulse-fit criteria for their ICP with multiple independent signals.
- Creator ecosystems and challenge behavior are **already in motion** and would benefit from better tooling, not net-new behavior creation.
- Community hub(s) are active, with consistent participation over at least the last 3–6 months.
- There is an obvious, pitch-ready concept for a **Pulse-integrated flagship activation** (e.g., creator-led challenge, data-driven program, or co-branded season).

### Future-fit

A partner is **Future-fit** when:

- They show **partial alignment** (strong brand/community fit) but are missing one or more structural elements (e.g., nascent creator ecosystem, irregular challenges, unclear community hub).
- There is credible evidence that the missing pieces could be developed **within 6–12 months** with the right support.
- They are interesting enough to track, nurture, or test in smaller experiments, but not yet ready for a flagship partnership narrative.

## How to Use This Document in Later Steps

- **Step 2 (Candidate Pool):** Use these criteria as a **screening checklist** to quickly discard misaligned or purely aesthetic candidates.
- **Step 3 (Behavioral Signals):** For shortlisted candidates, explicitly map findings back to the criteria (creator ecosystem, challenge behavior, community locus).
- **Step 4 (Fit Tiering):** Use the Flagship-ready vs Future-fit definitions above to tag each candidate, calling out disconfirming evidence explicitly.
- **Step 5 (A-list Docs):** Ensure each A-list profile includes a short note tying the partner back to these criteria, so the community docs stay anchored in behavioral reality, not vibes.

# Pulse-fit Community Partner Fit Tiering — Shortlisted Candidates (Step 4/5)

**Scope.** This document tags each shortlisted candidate from the behavioral signals pass with a **fit tier**:

- **Flagship-ready (Hypothesis)** — Strong behavioral alignment with Pulse-fit criteria *and* mature challenge/community patterns that Pulse could plug into quickly.
- **Future-fit (Hypothesis)** — Partial alignment, promising but with gaps in creator maturity, challenge consistency, or community hub clarity.

Because live web tooling is unavailable, all tiering is explicitly labeled **Hypothesis** until we can attach source-cited evidence. This doc is a **prioritization heuristic**, not a prospect status file.

---

## 1. Brands — Fit Tiering

Shortlist: Nike Run Club, Adidas Runners, WHOOP, Oura, Strava, Peloton.

### 1.1 Nike Run Club (NRC)

- **Proposed Tier:** **Flagship-ready (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Mature, global **creator + coach ecosystem** with city leaders and athlete storytelling.
  - Longstanding **training-plan and challenge structure** in the app with race-calibrated blocks.
  - Clear community hubs: NRC app + local run clubs + social handles.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Integration complexity and brand size may make near-term partnership unrealistic.
  - Training-plan completion patterns are still **Unverified**; actual finish-rates may be modest.

### 1.2 Adidas Runners

- **Proposed Tier:** **Flagship-ready (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Strong **captain-led community model**; captains double as creators and community managers.
  - Multi-week training blocks and race-season cycles provide clear Pulse challenge hooks.
  - City-level hubs with IG + chat groups suggest robust local coordination.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Local implementation quality likely varies; some chapters might be dormant.
  - Evidence for join vs finish dynamics still **Unverified**.

### 1.3 WHOOP

- **Proposed Tier:** **Flagship-ready (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Deep **data-first creator ecosystem**; creators already use WHOOP metrics to frame stories.
  - Built-in **team and challenge primitives** (leaderboards, group strain challenges).
  - High relevance to Pulse’s performance + recovery narratives.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Team engagement sustainability is **Unverified**; some groups may be short-lived.
  - Integration complexity (APIs, data privacy) to be validated.

### 1.4 Oura

- **Proposed Tier:** **Future-fit (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Strong **sleep and recovery storytelling** from creators; habit-focused, which matches Pulse.
  - Evidence of periodic habit challenges but less clear on multi-cohort, creator-led challenge depth.
  - Community hubs are more diffuse (app + creators) vs centralized challenge spaces.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Structured, multi-cohort challenge infrastructure is **Unverified**; may lean more toward individual experiments.
  - Need proof of cohort-level adherence and not just single-creator experiments.

### 1.5 Strava

- **Proposed Tier:** **Flagship-ready (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Strava is essentially a **social challenge and club infrastructure**: segments, monthly challenges, clubs.
  - Large, active **creator-equivalent population** (users posting activities, long captions, clubs as organizers).
  - Clear community hubs: app feed, clubs, segments, and challenge pages.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Many users passively join challenges; true completion vs enrollment is **Unverified**.
  - Overlap with existing Strava features could blur the value story unless Pulse adds something distinct.

### 1.6 Peloton

- **Proposed Tier:** **Future-fit (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Extremely strong **instructor-creator ecosystem**, with members amplifying.
  - Structured programs and occasional challenges provide clear adherence scaffolding.
  - Strongest community is inside Peloton’s own ecosystem; external integrations may be constrained.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Platform is relatively closed; embedding Pulse at depth may be challenging.
  - Data on program adherence and challenge completion is **Unverified**.

## 2. Gyms / Studios — Fit Tiering

Shortlist: Barry’s Bootcamp, Orangetheory, F45, Boxing studio archetype, Solidcore/reformer archetype, inclusive CrossFit box archetype.

### 2.1 Barry’s Bootcamp

- **Proposed Tier:** **Future-fit (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Strong **brand + instructor creators** and seasonal challenge culture.
  - Visual, high-energy identity is partnership-friendly for creator-led campaigns.
  - Community is meaningful but more **campaign-spiky** than always-on.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Depth of member accountability frameworks (beyond class packs + merch rewards) is **Unverified**.
  - Some locations may run challenges infrequently.

### 2.2 Orangetheory Fitness

- **Proposed Tier:** **Flagship-ready (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Built around **metrics and structured benchmarks** (Dri-Tri, benchmark days, global challenges).
  - Data-driven environment (HR zones, splat points) aligns cleanly with Pulse’s behavior tracking.
  - Global footprint with **repeated, systematized challenges**.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Corporate alignment and tech integration paths are **Unverified**.
  - Member experience is already heavily instrumented; need evidence that Pulse adds incremental value.

### 2.3 F45 Training

- **Proposed Tier:** **Future-fit (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Signature 6–8 week challenges provide a clear Pulse-like container.
  - Strong social amplification during challenge windows.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Challenge framing often leans into aesthetic transformation; brand alignment with Pulse’s health stance needs validation.
  - Quality and culture vary franchise-to-franchise.

### 2.4 Boxing Studio Archetype (Rumble-style)

- **Proposed Tier:** **Future-fit (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Instructor-centric, visually compelling classes; easier to build creator-led campaigns.
  - Some evidence of attendance or streak challenges.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Depth and consistency of structured challenges is **Unverified**.
  - Community may be more vibe- and event-driven than habit-structured.

### 2.5 Solidcore / Reformer Studio Archetype

- **Proposed Tier:** **Future-fit (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Members often track class counts and progression; clear hooks for streak- and milestone-based Pulse challenges.
  - Smaller but dedicated communities that appreciate structure.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Formal challenge infrastructure (beyond “N classes in a month”) is **Unverified**.
  - Studio tech stack and data access for integration unknown.

### 2.6 Inclusive CrossFit Box Archetype

- **Proposed Tier:** **Flagship-ready (Hypothesis)** (for selected boxes)
- **Why:**
  - Strong **community identity** with recurring events (Open, throwdowns) and coach-led programming.
  - Members are already accustomed to logging workouts and celebrating milestones.
  - Many boxes have **private group hubs** (FB/WhatsApp/Slack), mapping cleanly to Pulse’s community primitives.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Box culture varies widely; need careful selection for inclusive, safety-aligned partners.
  - Participation depth in challenges beyond The Open is **Unverified**.

## 3. Run Clubs — Fit Tiering

Shortlist: Nike Run Club (city), Adidas Runners (city), November Project, Urban run crews, Tracksmith/amateur collectives, Charity/cause-based run club.

### 3.1 Nike Run Club — City Chapter Archetype

- **Proposed Tier:** **Flagship-ready (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Weekly runs and race build-ups mirror Pulse’s cohort-challenge model.
  - Clear leadership roles (captains/coaches) and community identity.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Individual chapter health varies; some may be dormant.
  - Integration at Nike brand level is complex.

### 3.2 Adidas Runners — City Club Archetype

- **Proposed Tier:** **Flagship-ready (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Highly structured **pace groups + captains** with race-specific training cycles.
  - Strong fit for city-based, creator-led Pulse pilots.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Same as NRC: variation by city; some may not maintain consistent cadence.

### 3.3 November Project

- **Proposed Tier:** **Future-fit (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Culture-first, free fitness with very strong identity and accountability norms.
  - Works well for attendance and streak-based narratives.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Volunteer-led structure may make technology integrations slower/messier.
  - Some cities may have waning participation; schedule consistency is **Unverified**.

### 3.4 Urban Run Crews (Bridge Runners / Black Roses / Run Dem Crew Archetype)

- **Proposed Tier:** **Future-fit (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Deep culture and storytelling; strong brand collab potential.
  - Training blocks and race prep exist but are often less formally codified.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Crews may be protective of culture; need alignment proof before pushing structured, app-mediated challenges.
  - Some crews might resist anything that feels overly productized.

### 3.5 Tracksmith / Amateur Racing Collectives Archetype

- **Proposed Tier:** **Flagship-ready (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Structured training cycles and serious amateur cohorts.
  - Strong alignment with performance storytelling and race-focused seasons.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Must validate inclusivity and not over-index on performance-at-all-costs.

### 3.6 Charity / Cause-based Run Club Archetype

- **Proposed Tier:** **Future-fit (Hypothesis)**
- **Why:**
  - Extremely strong intrinsic motivation and storytelling around cause.
  - Training cycles and fundraising milestones map well to Pulse challenge structures.
- **Disconfirming / Risk Notes:**
  - Cause alignment and brand safety require case-by-case diligence.
  - Tech/data environment varies by charity; integration complexity **Unverified**.

  - A **nurture list** for future experiments once evidence and structure catch up.
- Every A-list profile should:
  1. Reference the behavioral signals file for specific creator/challenge evidence.
  2. Carry over the **Hypothesis** label until we have at least one verified, source-cited behavioral example.
  3. Explicitly restate **disconfirming signals** to keep our partnership narratives honest and grounded.

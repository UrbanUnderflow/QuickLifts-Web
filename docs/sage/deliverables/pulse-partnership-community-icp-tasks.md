# Pulse Partnership Community ICP Tasks

## 1. Partnership Strategy Spine

Pulse’s next growth phase is powered by partnerships that act as true extensions of the product, not ad slots: we prioritize 3–5 strategic collaborations with fitness, wellness, and wearable brands whose products, communities, and values naturally interlock with Pulse so that every partnership amplifies our creator ecosystem, drives in-app engagement through co-created experiences, and generates revenue to fund product development while clearing a high bar for health integrity and trust.

### Core ICPs and Primary Goals for Pulse

- **Brands (supplements, apparel, wellness products)**  
  **Primary goal for Pulse:** Amplify and legitimize creator-led campaigns by pairing aligned products with credible, habit-building programming inside Pulse, so campaigns feel like integrated training arcs rather than one-off sponsorship posts.

- **Gyms & Studios**  
  **Primary goal for Pulse:** Digitize and extend their in-house communities through Pulse-powered programming, challenges, and check-ins that keep members connected to coaches and each other between sessions, increasing retention and visit frequency.

- **Run Clubs & Endurance Communities**  
  **Primary goal for Pulse:** Scale their core rituals (routes, weekly sessions, race blocks, accountability threads) into a structured, trackable digital layer that deepens community identity, improves training consistency, and opens up co-created events and challenges with Pulse creators and brand partners.

## 2. ICP-Specific Playbook Deliverables

### 2.1 Brands Playbook Deliverables

- **Brands ICP Brief**  
  A 2–3 page internal brief that defines the ideal brand partner profile (categories, values, regulatory posture), audience overlap with Pulse, and partnership archetypes (e.g., challenge sponsors, supplement integrations, capsule programs). Used primarily by **Nora** and **partnership sales** to qualify targets and shape outbound narratives.

- **Brands Hero Community Narrative**  
  A story-driven, one-page narrative that follows a creator + brand + Pulse user arc ("from scattered promos to integrated training block") to make the value of partnership tangible. Used by **Solara** in pitch decks and by **Nora** when aligning product and campaign concepts.

- **Brands Behavior Metrics Snapshot**  
  A focused 1–2 page metrics sheet highlighting creator-driven engagement (e.g., program completion rates, challenge participation, repeat workout behavior) that proves Pulse can sustain brand campaigns beyond a single post. Produced by **Scout** and used by **Nora** and **partnership sales** in decks and one-pagers.

- **Brands Language & Claims Guardrails**  
  A concise, scenario-based guardrail doc that outlines allowed vs. prohibited language for supplements, recovery tools, and wellness products inside Pulse (e.g., performance vs. disease claims), plus examples of acceptable co-branded copy. Owned by **Sage** and used by **Solara**, **creators**, and **partnership sales** when drafting campaigns.

### 2.2 Gyms & Studios Playbook Deliverables

- **Gyms & Studios ICP Brief**  
  A 2–3 page brief that defines ideal gym/studio partners (modality, member demographics, retention challenges), maps how Pulse extends their existing offerings, and details integration patterns (e.g., member onboarding flows, hybrid programming). Used by **Nora** and **B2B partnership owners** to target and structure conversations.

- **Gyms Hero Community Narrative**  
  A narrative that follows a specific gym community from "classes as isolated events" to a continuous, Pulse-supported training ecosystem with check-ins, challenges, and coach-led progress stories. Used by **Solara** in sales narratives and by **gym-facing CSMs** to explain the vision to staff.

- **Gyms Behavior Metrics Snapshot**  
  A metrics view focused on retention and engagement signals relevant to gyms (e.g., streak days, session adherence, challenge completion, member check-ins) that demonstrate how Pulse can drive visit frequency and reduce churn. Produced by **Scout** and used by **Nora**, CSMs, and **partnership sales** in pitches and onboarding.

- **Gyms Language & Claims Guardrails**  
  A guardrail document tailored to gym/studio use cases that clarifies how we talk about results, injury risk, and coach qualifications across co-branded programs, plus how we handle before/after framing. Owned by **Sage** and used by **Solara**, **CSMs**, and **gym partners** when configuring programs and marketing copy.

### 2.3 Run Clubs & Endurance Communities Playbook Deliverables

- **Run Clubs ICP Brief**  
  A 2–3 page profile of ideal run club and endurance community partners (size, cadence, culture, race calendar), outlining how Pulse supports their rituals (routes, weekly runs, training blocks) and where co-created programs and challenges fit. Used by **Nora** and **community/partnership owners** to prioritize and pitch.

- **Run Clubs Hero Community Narrative**  
  A narrative that follows a run club from loosely organized group chats to a cohesive, trackable training ecosystem in Pulse with shared milestones, race prep blocks, and partner activations. Used by **Solara** in partnership materials and by **community leads** to explain the Pulse layer to their runners.

- **Run Clubs Behavior Metrics Snapshot**  
  A metrics sheet focused on endurance-specific behaviors (e.g., completion of training blocks, long-run adherence, group challenge engagement, pre-race vs. post-race activity) that shows how Pulse strengthens consistency and community identity. Produced by **Scout** and used in **Nora’s** strategy work and **partnership sales** decks.

- **Run Clubs Language & Claims Guardrails**  
  A targeted guardrail doc covering performance claims, injury-prevention language, and race-related promises across co-branded run programming, with examples for creators and partners. Owned by **Sage** and used by **Solara**, **creators**, and **run club partners** when writing program descriptions, announcements, and challenge copy.

## 3. Granular Tasks by ICP & Deliverable

### 3.1 Brands – Granular Tasks

#### 3.1.1 Brands ICP Brief

- **Task: Map ideal brand partner profile**  
  - Description: Synthesize categories (supplements, apparel, recovery, wellness tech), value alignment, regulatory posture, and audience overlap into a clear partner profile (who is in, who is out, and why).  
  - Required inputs: Existing partnership strategy notes, any current or past brand conversations (Unverified unless backed by SOT), Pulse brand manifesto, legal/regulatory notes on health claims.  
  - Owner: **Sage** (lead synthesis) with review by **Nora**.

- **Task: Define partnership archetypes and use cases**  
  - Description: Enumerate 3–5 concrete partnership archetypes (e.g., 8-week creator-led supplement-supported block, apparel x challenge collab) with example flows inside Pulse.  
  - Required inputs: Creator program examples, existing or aspirational campaign concepts, product surfaces in the app where brand integrations are natural.  
  - Owner: **Nora** (structures archetypes) with narrative refinement by **Solara**.

- **Task: Write decision-ready ICP brief draft**  
  - Description: Turn profile + archetypes into a 2–3 page, skimmable brief that a partnerships lead can actually use to qualify targets and decide “yes/no” quickly.  
  - Required inputs: Outputs from previous two tasks, examples of target and non-target brands, feedback from at least one mock qualification pass.  
  - Owner: **Sage** (draft) with sign-off by **Nora**.

#### 3.1.2 Brands Hero Community Narrative

- **Task: Select anchor creator + user storyline**  
  - Description: Choose a representative creator persona and user journey that illustrates how a brand integration becomes a coherent training arc, not a one-off promo.  
  - Required inputs: Creator personas, sample program flows, anecdotal stories from existing Pulse users or creators (real or archetypal, clearly marked).  
  - Owner: **Sage** (pattern selection) with doors-open input from **Scout** on behavior patterns.

- **Task: Draft hero narrative (one-page story)**  
  - Description: Write a one-page narrative (setup → tension → resolution) showing the before/after of a creator-led brand partnership inside Pulse.  
  - Required inputs: Approved storyline, brand archetype details, tone and voice guidelines from Solara.  
  - Owner: **Solara** (primary writer) with health-claim review by **Sage**.

- **Task: Extract reusable story beats and quotes**  
  - Description: Turn the narrative into 3–5 modular beats (e.g., “activation moment”, “habit stickiness proof”) and 2–3 sample quotes that can be dropped into decks and one-pagers.  
  - Required inputs: Final hero narrative, pitch deck templates, existing partnership one-liners.  
  - Owner: **Sage** (extraction) with framing tweaks by **Solara**.

#### 3.1.3 Brands Behavior Metrics Snapshot

- **Task: Define brand-relevant engagement metrics**  
  - Description: Select a small, sharp set of metrics that matter to brands (e.g., completion rate of sponsored programs, multi-touch engagement over a campaign window).  
  - Required inputs: Current telemetry schema, historical engagement data, partner-question patterns from past pitches.  
  - Owner: **Scout** (metric selection) with prioritization input from **Nora**.

- **Task: Pull and validate baseline data**  
  - Description: Run queries to pull the selected metrics, validate them for accuracy, and annotate any caveats (sample size, seasonality, feature flags).  
  - Required inputs: Production or staging analytics access, metric definitions, known data quality notes.  
  - Owner: **Scout**.

- **Task: Assemble metrics snapshot document**  
  - Description: Present metrics in a 1–2 page, partner-friendly format with short interpretations (“what this means for your campaign”).  
  - Required inputs: Clean metrics table, brand ICP brief, design/writing pattern from existing pitch materials.  
  - Owner: **Sage** (interpretation) with visual polish by **Solara**.

#### 3.1.4 Brands Language & Claims Guardrails

- **Task: Inventory common brand claim patterns**  
  - Description: List the types of claims brand partners and creators are likely to make (performance, recovery, body composition, mood, etc.), and map them against regulatory risk.  
  - Required inputs: Existing creator copy, example brand campaigns (Unverified unless linked to SOT evidence), legal summaries on supplement and wellness marketing.  
  - Owner: **Sage** with optional consultation from legal/compliance.

- **Task: Draft red/green language examples for each claim type**  
  - Description: For each claim category, write “green” phrasing (acceptable) and “red” phrasing (avoid), plus rationale where needed.  
  - Required inputs: Inventory from previous task, Pulse brand guidelines, any prior compliance feedback.  
  - Owner: **Sage**.

- **Task: Package guardrails into creator-friendly format**  
  - Description: Turn the guardrails into a concise, scannable guide (bullets, examples) that creators and sales can actually use when drafting copy.  
  - Required inputs: Draft guardrail content, knowledge of creator workflows, input from **Solara** on tone.  
  - Owner: **Solara** (formatting and tone) with content ownership by **Sage**.

  - Required inputs: Sensitive areas list, Pulse tone guide, any prior compliance guidance.

- **Task: Build a short guardrail checklist for CSMs and gyms**  
  - Description: Turn guardrails into a checklist CSMs can walk through with gym partners when reviewing co-branded materials.

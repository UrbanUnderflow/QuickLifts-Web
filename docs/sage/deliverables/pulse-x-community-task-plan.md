# Pulse x Community — Task Plan

This is the execution checklist for the Pulse x Community narrative and ICP strategy work. Each item has a clear owner, concrete acceptance criteria, and ICP-specific expectations so the team can ship without rereading the original brainstorm.

---

## 1. Core Narrative Spine

**Goal:** Ship a single, reusable "Pulse x Community" narrative paragraph (+ minimal framing) that anchors all partnership materials.

- [ ] **Draft core narrative spine**  
  **Owner:** Solara (DRI)  
  **Support (as needed):** Nora, Scout
  - [ ] Write a 1–2 paragraph narrative that explains how Pulse turns existing communities (brands, gyms, run clubs) into living, trackable training ecosystems.  
  - [ ] Read it out loud and revise until it sounds like a real coach/creator talking to another human — no generic SaaS or fitness wallpaper phrases like "unlock your potential" or "crush your goals".  
  - [ ] Explicitly reference at least two concrete community behaviors (e.g., challenge join/finish/return rates, creator-led blocks, streak weeks) instead of abstract "engagement".  
  - [ ] Make a clear, emotionally resonant claim about why Pulse treats community differently than other fitness apps (without naming competitors or copying their language).

**Output Artifact:** Core narrative section in the shared narrative doc (or a dedicated `Pulse x Community Narrative` section in the deck/one-pager source).

---

## 2. KPI Specification

**Goal:** Define the small set of KPIs that prove Pulse is working for a partner community, with definitions and example baselines.

- [ ] **Define Pulse x Community KPI spec**  
  **Owner:** Nora (DRI)  
  **Support (as needed):** Scout (data), Solara (voice)
  - [ ] Select 5–8 KPIs that map directly to observable community behaviors (e.g., new challenge joins, block completion, streak weeks, creator activation/retention).  
  - [ ] Write plain-language descriptions for each KPI so a smart, non-technical partner can understand them without seeing the schema.  
  - [ ] Provide at least one example baseline or target range per KPI (even if illustrative) to make the numbers feel real.
  - [ ] For **Brands**, flag KPIs most relevant to marketing/community teams (e.g., repeat challenge participation, UGC volume, creator activation/retention) and mark them for inclusion in the Brands ICP doc.  
  - [ ] For **Gyms & Studios**, flag KPIs like block fill rate, block completion, member return across blocks, hybrid (IRL + remote) check-in consistency for the Gyms/Studios ICP doc.  
  - [ ] For **Run Clubs**, flag KPIs such as route/meetup attendance trends, progression from casual runs to race blocks, and streak weeks across a season for the Run Clubs ICP doc.

**Output Artifact:** KPI spec section/document that can be dropped into decks and referenced by all three ICP docs.

---

## 3. Partner-Fit Rubric (Pulse-fit Community Partners)

**Goal:** Create a behavior-based rubric to qualify whether a prospect is truly Pulse-fit across Brands, Gyms/Studios, and Run Clubs.

- [ ] **Draft Pulse-fit partner rubric**  
  **Owner:** Scout (DRI)  
  **Support (as needed):** Solara (values/voice), Nora (strategy/risk)
  - [ ] Define 3–5 rubric dimensions that are explicitly behavioral (e.g., challenge cadence, creator activity, return participation, clarity of community home), each with a short description.  
  - [ ] For **Brands**, add example signals such as: recurring ambassador/creator programs with drops, history of challenge-based campaigns, active community channels with workout/recipe/race content, clear home for the community (Discord, IG, app, etc.).  
  - [ ] For **Gyms & Studios**, add example signals such as: recurring named training blocks, ongoing coach-led accountability threads or check-ins, evidence of multi-block returners, clear cross-channel communication (in-person + digital).  
  - [ ] For **Run Clubs**, add example signals such as: seasonal training calendars, consistent weekly route schedules, race-focused blocks, visible streak/consistency culture, and a defined communication hub.  
  - [ ] Write the rubric in plain language so a new teammate can screen a prospect after one read.

**Output Artifact:** Partner-fit rubric document/section that can be used in prospect screening and a one-slide summary in decks.

---

## 4. Brands ICP Document

**Goal:** Turn the shared narrative, KPIs, and rubric into a brands-specific story that a senior marketing/community lead would say yes to.

- [ ] **Draft Brands ICP narrative**  
  **Owner:** Solara (DRI)  
  **Support (as needed):** Nora (integration feasibility), Scout (behavior → metric alignment)
  - [ ] Write a narrative that shows how Pulse powers creator- and ambassador-led drops and challenges that feel like the community is alive, not like sponsored wallpaper.  
  - [ ] Include at least two vivid, concrete examples of brand x Pulse activations (e.g., a 14-day ambassador-led reset tied to a product launch; a challenge-based campaign with a UGC recap moment).  
  - [ ] Explicitly tie brand outcomes (e.g., repeat participation, content volume, challenge return rates) to 2–3 KPIs from the shared spec.
  - [ ] Call out a brand-owned "season" pattern (e.g., summer strength, winter recovery) where Pulse challenges structure the arc of content and participation.
  - [ ] Ensure the tone reads like a conversation with a senior brand/community lead — specific, respectful, no hypey or condescending marketing language.
  - [ ] Confirm the examples align with Pulse-fit rubric signals for brands (creator programs, challenge behavior, clear community home).

**Output Artifact:** Brands ICP doc/section that can be dropped into brand partnership decks and one-pagers.

---

## 5. Gyms & Studios ICP Document

**Goal:** Tell a gym/studio story that makes sense to owners and head coaches who live inside blocks, check-ins, and member retention.

- [ ] **Draft Gyms & Studios ICP narrative**  
  **Owner:** Solara (DRI)  
  **Support (as needed):** Nora (product flows), Scout (behavior → metric alignment)
  - [ ] Describe at least two concrete flows a gym cares about (e.g., filling an 8–12 week training block, keeping members through deload weeks, tracking check-ins across IRL and remote) and how Pulse supports each.  
  - [ ] Tie those flows to 2–3 gym-relevant KPIs (e.g., block fill/completion rate, member return across blocks, check-in consistency, streak weeks) using specific example numbers or patterns where possible.  
  - [ ] Include at least one hybrid participation example where in-person classes and Pulse-based homework sessions both count toward streaks and completion.
  - [ ] Use language that sounds like coaches and owners actually describe their people and programming, not like a SaaS sales script.  
  - [ ] Check that the examples reflect Pulse-fit rubric signals for gyms (named blocks, accountability behavior, repeat participation, cross-channel comms).

**Output Artifact:** Gyms & Studios ICP doc/section for gym-focused decks and one-pagers.

---

## 6. Run Clubs ICP Document

**Goal:** Give run club leaders a story that feels like it understands seasons, routes, races, and the unpaid labor of running a club.

- [ ] **Draft Run Clubs ICP narrative**  
  **Owner:** Solara (DRI)  
  **Support (as needed):** Nora (product surfaces), Scout (behavior → metric alignment)
  - [ ] Include at least one seasonal training arc (e.g., "Spring Base → Summer Speed → Fall Marathon") mapped as sequential Pulse blocks with runners carrying their history forward.  
  - [ ] Describe a weekly route pattern (e.g., Tuesday tempo, Saturday long run with rotating routes) and how Pulse tracks who shows up over a season.  
  - [ ] Add at least one streak or consistency mechanic (e.g., "4-week consistency streak", "no-zero-week season") that’s visible to both runners and captains.
  - [ ] Tie club activity to 2–3 shared KPIs (e.g., route/meetup attendance trends, progression from casual runs into race blocks, streak weeks across a cycle) with at least one concrete example trend.  
  - [ ] Write it in a way a run club captain would actually forward to co-leads — clear, specific, and respectful of their unpaid organizing work.  
  - [ ] Verify that examples align with Pulse-fit rubric signals for run clubs (seasonal structure, consistent routes, race blocks, streak culture, clear comms hub).

**Output Artifact:** Run Clubs ICP doc/section for run club decks and one-pagers.

# Hunter World: Leveling & Leaderboards

## The System
The Hunter World leveling system is built on **Pulse Points**, converting real-world athletic effort into a tiered ranking system heavily inspired by the Solo Leveling narrative.

### The Ranks
1. **E-Rank:** 0 - 999 pts (Novice)
2. **D-Rank:** 1,000 - 4,999 pts (Amateur)
3. **C-Rank:** 5,000 - 14,999 pts (Intermediate)
4. **B-Rank:** 15,000 - 39,999 pts (Advanced)
5. **A-Rank:** 40,000 - 99,999 pts (Elite)
6. **S-Rank:** 100,000+ pts (Master)

Points are permanent (`lifetimePulsePoints`) and serve as a cumulative record of all workouts, runs, fat burns, and challenges completed within the Pulse ecosystem.

## Rank Maintenance (Pulse Power)
To ensure ranks remain meaningful and competitive, users must maintain a minimum 30-day activity threshold, known as **Pulse Power**. 
- If Pulse Power drops below the required maintenance threshold for a user's current rank, they enter a probation period.
- Continued inactivity results in rank demotion (though lifetime points remain untouched). 
- *Note: S-Rank is designed to be exceedingly difficult to maintain, requiring near-daily high-intensity engagement.*

## The Global Leaderboard
The leaderboard is segmented by rank tier to foster competitive engagement at all levels.
- **Your Rank:** Compare yourself directly against users in your current tier (e.g., "Top B-Rank Hunters").
- **Global Top 100:** Absolute ranking of the entire platform (dominated by S and A ranks).
- **The Rising:** Weekly views highlighting users who have gained the most points in the last 7 days, allowing new users to gain visibility. 

## Competitive Mechanics
- **Bounties:** Weekly automated milestones (e.g., "Run 10 miles this week") that award bonus points.
- **Gate Events:** Month-long, community-wide challenges against "Bosses" (milestones) heavily pushed by Creators.
- **Duels:** 1v1 asynchronous point battles between users.
